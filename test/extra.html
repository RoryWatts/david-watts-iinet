this week:Cricket


CRICKET is unfathomnable to most people outside the Commonwealth, but to those countries colonised by the British it has come to be as important as life itself.
The game began on the playing fields of public schools and was first written about in the 1600s.

THE game was virtually unchanged from the 19th century to the 1970s when Australian business tycoon Kerry Packer began a rebel competition ... World Series Cricket.
Now one-day play dominates TV schedules if not purists' hearts.
