"Loose cannon' Drudge gets a radio chat show 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 13-07-1999

 INTERNET users have applauded the move of Matt Drudge - the world's highest-profile online journalist - to the more traditional medium of radio.
Drudge, 32, the man who broke the Monica Lewinsky story, has signed a deal with US company ABC Radio Networks to do a weekly talk show.
Drudge, who first posted the Lewinsky sex allegations on his Web site in January 1998, will host a two-hour topical commentary and listener call-in show. His ABC salary is said to be in six figures.
According to a report in the London online edition of the Guardian, the deal was reached only over the strenuous objections of the head of the ABC news division, David Westin.
Mr Westin was reported to have depicted Drudge as a loose cannon already facing a $50 million libel suit for falsely accusing a White House aide, Sydney Blumenthal, of beating his wife - a claim Drudge quickly retracted.
Howard Kurtz, the Washington Post's media correspondent, said the ABC deal saw ?the further blurring of the news and entertainment cultures. What's fascinating is that a rebel and an outsider armed with nothing more than a modem can break into this rather exclusive club.?
But the majority of Net users, who joined a BBC e-mail forum about Drudge's move, welcomed the fact that Drudge's style of gossip-cum-news had been accepted in the so-called real world.
Among the many comments from around the world were:
? ?He is a genuine, honest, journalist. The only one I've seen who does his homework.?
??Drudge breaks stories before the mainstream media do, if they mention it at all.?
??Hardly a gossip monger, I find the Drudge Report to be cutting-edge journalism. More often than not, he is the first to report news that eventually (and often begrudgingly) comes out on television and radio news.?
? ?When will the established media realise, "the times, they are a changing'. We now live in microwave times. As network TV gave way to cable, newspapers will give way to the Internet.?
??America needs more men like Drudge. Stand up and "tell it like it is'.?
Drudge's Web site, called the Drudge Report, is extremely popular. It claims to have had more than 20 million visits from Web surfers in the past month.
Little of its content is created by Drudge himself. His often pithy - and sensational - daily column represents a tiny percentage of the material presented. Instead, Drudge operates a global media village with dozens of links to wire services, newspapers, radio stations, television news and emerging Net-only news services.
Drudge says he culls his report from 35 daily newspapers, wire services and more than 1000 daily e-mail tips. Recently his style of online reporting was described as being on the cutting edge of a revolution in journalism.
Last month, Drudge earned the right to address the National Press Club in Washington. It seems his often-entertaining responses to accusations of rumour-mongering and sensationalism were well received by the mainstream media.
