It's your shout for serious research 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 22-01-1999

 THIS heat is enough to drive a man to drink. Much as I would like to do some practical research into that claim, I am afraid I am bound by the politically correct strictures of modern times.
That is: I cannot go out, drink until I can no longer talk, then come back to the computer and write a column starting with the words ?I'm as skunk as a drunk and here ish what happens when you've had more to think then you drink you have, hic.?
No, can't do that these days. How fondly I remember my early days in newspapers, before the dark clouds of booze buses, health fanatics and tut-tutters.
In 1975 I was given a dream assignment by my country daily. ?David, there is a beer strike. Find out how it's affecting the town.?
Whacko. I found my mate Thomo and we proceeded to do research into the beer strike by going on a pub crawl. The town had 15 pubs so we decided not to be greedy and have only two middies at each pub.
By the eighth pub, we had quite forgotten why we were on a pub crawl and decided to stay in the lounge bar for the weekly cabaret.
After an hour, the barmaid said the pub had run out of beer and we were forced to change to rum. ?Why ishn't there any beer?? I asked. ?Because there's a beer strike,? said the barmaid. ?Geez, stuff like that should be in the paper. Waddya reckon Thomo?? I said.
Thomo looked up from his glass and said: ?I'm good with beer. I know when I've had enough.?
He promptly fell off his bar stool and muttered: ?I've had enough.?
As hard as it might be to believe, I did file a report on the beer strike for the next day's paper, although some of the more emotive content was edited out.
The words ?there's no bloody beer?, ?don't panic? and ?still plenty of good rum? failed to make it.
Ah, the good, old days. You cannot do research like that any more, so I had to rely on the Internet.
Here I found a site written by a man from Ecuador in both Spanish and English which presented the surprising fact that more than 99 per cent of dead people have been found to have drunk water; whereas only 64 per cent had been found to have consumed alcohol.
The site then went on to examine some common problems experienced while drinking, and offered solutions.
For example:
Problem - Drinking fails to give taste and satisfaction, beer is unusually pale and clear.
Probable cause - Glass empty.
Suggested action - Find someone who will buy you another beer.
Or:
Problem - Drinking fails to give taste and satisfaction, and the front of your shirt is wet.
Cause - Mouth not open when drinking or glass applied to wrong part of face.
Action - Buy another beer and practise in front of mirror. Drink as many as needed to perfect drinking technique.
And we must not forget the impression alcohol has made on some great minds (and many not so great) through the ages:
Always remember that I have taken more out of alcohol than alcohol has taken out of me - Winston Churchill.
A woman drove me to drink and I didn't even have the decency to thank her - WC Fields.
Beer is proof that God loves us and wants us to be happy - Benjamin Franklin.
You're not drunk if you can lie on the floor without holding on - Dean Martin.
I'll drink to those.
