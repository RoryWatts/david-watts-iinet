New sites highlight WA Web popularity 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 18-06-1996

 THE enthusiasm West Australians have for the World Wide Web is evident in two new, quite different sites.
Both are directories of purely WA Web sites.
If you can say something as young as the Internet has a tradition then the WA Web Index is following that with its straightforward presentation of sites.
Developed by I.T. Business Solutions and Bit-Media, the index allows surfers to call up a list of new sites submitted to the index in the past week or so. Each site has its name and a small synopsis written by the person who submitted it.
Bit-Media is using it as a showcase for some of its own sites in these early days, but there is a growing variety.
At a time when gun owners are being shot to pieces it is interesting to see an online petition calling for legalisation of paintball - the mock shoot-em-up game - in WA.
The petition has been signed via e-mail by hundreds of people from around the world, many asking is WA serious in not allowing what they consider a harmless recreation.
The other new directory is vastly different. WA@RANDOM, developed by E Media, operates like a roulette wheel, except that you click ?go? and never know exactly where you will end up.
The one certainty is that it will be at a WA site. In only a few weeks WA@RANDOM has attracted nearly 800 submissions. There is a good scattering of E Media-related sites, but plenty of others.
