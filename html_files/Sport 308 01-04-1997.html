Footy feast is top television 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 01-04-1997

 CHANNEL 7 should rate Fremantle matches MA, for adults only. Rather than a V for violence, S for sex scenes or A for adult themes, the games should attract a new tag - K for kicking.
Children, particularly those learning to kick straight, should not be allowed to watch Fremantle's all-too-regular botch-ups in front of goal.
At Optus Oval on Saturday, the Dockers showed what footy excitement is all about. That's excitement as in heart- attack material.
Gerard Neesham's boys should have had the Western Bulldogs squared away by the third quarter but they had to wait until Winston Abraham's freakish 60m winner in the last minute.
Not that TV viewers needed a Fremantle heart-stopper to excite them in this feast of AFL football.
It started in the MCG rain on Thursday night with Melbourne beating premier North Melbourne.
ABC radio commentator Drew Morphett was heard to say that North's 7.13 was a Docker-like score. The Roos will have to try harder to match the real thing of 10.20.
From that shock opener to yesterday's magnificent Essendon victory over Carlton in front of 82,000 at a sunny MCG, it was TV sport at its very best.
Try these highlights and then wonder why anyone would want to buy pay-TV to watch rugby league:
?Peter Matera's boundary line-hugging goal against Sydney.
?Ben Cousins crashing into free space from nowhere to kick morale-boosting goals in the same game.
?The Subiaco crowd's heartfelt applause for Swan champion Paul Roos when he was taken off on a stretcher.
?Dennis Cometti's observation that Sydney champion Paul Kelly is so courageous he plays as if he is using someone else's body.
?Tony Modra leading at the speed of light against Brisbane and kicking seven.
?Matt Richardson's speed and Nick Daffy's snap-happy shooting to help Richmond pip Geelong.
?Essendon's amazing keep-it-offs that lasted two minutes, culminated in a goal and victory over Carlton.
In the not-so-happy section we had:
?The great complainer, Greg Williams, shoving an umpire aside in a bad-tempered outburst after the siren yesterday.
?A very silly layout for Seven's Footy Panel that had Gerard Healy grinning like a galoot as he snuggled up to Rex Hunt.
