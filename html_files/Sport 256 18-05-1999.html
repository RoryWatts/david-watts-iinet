Fans thrive on overs exposure 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 18-05-1999

 SPORT
Cricket World Cup
THE Web is covering the cricket World Cup in England with its usual respect for the game and its fascinating by-product of endless statistics.
If you do not choose to glue yourself to either Channel 9 or Fox Sports, these sites will keep you enlightened about the tournament.
Cricket Unlimited
www.cricketunlimited.co.uk/
Series/0,3118,-130,00.html THE Guardian's cricket site has a special section devoted to the Cup.
Its highlights include rapid publication of game summaries and analyses by respected cricket writers, plus links to Wisden.
Cricinfo
www-aus.cricket.org/link-to-database/
ARCHIVE/WORLD-CUP THE Web bible for cricket lovers gives its usual all-or-nothing approach.
This will be the best site to ?watch? constantly updated scores from all matches and read ball-by-ball commentaries.
The links to latest news and the huge database of stats are treats too.
Cricket.Net
wc99.hypermart.net
FOR those to whom cricket is not a way of life, this unofficial page offers plenty of reading and a chance to study the Cup in a more relaxed manner without being bombarded by scores, updates and the like.
POLITICS
Federal Budget
www.treasury.gov.au/budget/
THE Treasury's site is one of several that offer all this year's Budget papers for download.
It is chosen purely on the grounds that if the Treasury wants to give an accurate portrayal of facts and figures, then its own site will be well looked after.
All documents, including speeches and overview are in HTML, Adobe Acrobat or Rich Text format.
State Budget
www.treasury.wa.gov.au/budp9900.html
WEB users could be mistaken for thinking that the WA Budget is not being promoted online.
The hint that something is there is a link on Premier Richard Court's page which promises only a Budget speech.
But a click on this link takes you to a special Budget site where all the papers can be downloaded in Acrobat or MS Word format.
The Premier's site also links to press releases used to ?sell? the Budget.
