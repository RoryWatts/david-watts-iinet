Boys will be lads, dudes and blokes 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 14-10-1996

 YOU may not have noticed, but in the past five or six years big changes have been happening in sex. Not the actual act; that has remained much the same since Adam's voice broke. No, by sex, I mean gender.
Youths are wearing their testosterone on the outside more and more.
Very short razor haircuts, considered the essence of dagginess in the 1960s and 70s, are commonplace as are horrific shirts and baggy trousers.
Girls have cut with the feminine look in casual wear; lots of shorts, tanktops, short socks and heavy boots.
When they dress to kill, however, they show more flesh than their swinging 60s and 70s counterparts (often their own mothers) ever dared.
I am no expert, just an observer, but it appears to me our young people - and more than a few who have been through several fads - are asserting their sexuality like never before.
In the US, the phenomenon is known as dudes and babes, in the UK it's lads and lasses.
As far as I know no phrase has been coined for Australian youth strutting their stuff. Perhaps we should know them as we always have; blokes and sheilas.
The sensitive new age guy appears to have slunk out the back door ... to bring in the washing. In the front door, the bloke strolls with a twinkle in his eye for the sheilas, a big thirst and a penchant for sport.
The sheila greets him with a swagger, a glimpse at her navel ring and a demand to go mountain bike riding, long distance running or drinking down the pub to the roar of a rock band.
At the pub, blokes and sheilas both drink straight from the stubby ... no Tia Maria and milk or something with a giraffe stick and umbrella in it for today's girl.
It is quite refreshingly different from the days when a woman's place was in the home (50s), when the earth mother ruled but didn't actually make the bread, except the eating kind (60s and 70s), or when shoulder-padded, suit wearers strode down the Terrace like carnivores (the 80s).
It is a more honest approach to both the differences and similarities in men and women that gives rise to the 90s bloke and sheila.
And let's be honest, there are many differences.
Again I can only draw on my own observations (one-eyed though they may be). Here are just a few:
?When my daughter was five and my son two, she put on a Nativity play for her grandma using toys. It was sweet and lovely, but as Mary and Joseph rode a My Little Pony to the stable my son jumped on a couch in front of everyone, took down his pants and shouted ?look at my bottom.?
?A female friend who attended a convent told me the nuns used to warn the girls not to over-polish their shoes lest boys used the reflection to look at the girls' underwear.
To her horror, I told her this was a necessary precaution as boys, from about the age of 6 (when they don't know why) to about 96 (when they can't remember why) will do anything to look at a girl's knickers.
?Five years ago my senior high school class had a 20th reunion in Queensland which I couldn't attend because the swine of a chief sub-editor here had rostered me to work and I hadn't won Lotto (as usual).
The girls of the class organised the event, sending out notices and tracking all 52 students down all over the world a full year before the actual event, which was very successful, I'm told.
So successful that the boys said they would organise a 25th. That should have been this year and no one's heard a thing. Sheilas seem to be the only ones capable of getting events such as this organised.
