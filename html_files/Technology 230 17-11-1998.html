Ultimate updater banishes past, fast 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 17-11-1998

 IT IS fashionable on the Internet to refer to comprehensive Web sites as the ultimate. There is the Ultimate Band List and the Ultimate TV List, for example.
If the same theory were applied to software, Catch.UP would deserve the tag of ultimate helper.
The program works as a scanner application plugged into your browser, and can be activated only from its parent Web site.
It interrogates your hard disk to see what programs you have, then scours the Net for new versions or fixes. It returns a Web page listing available upgrades.
It does all this in less than two minutes.
Traditionally, users had to connect to several different software libraries and check higgledy-piggledy lists of the latest additions.
Catch.UP now does nearly all of it, making waiting for the download the most laborious part of an updating exercise.
The only time-consuming action is the initial set-up when you have to mark the programs on your hard disk that you want checked for newer versions.
Catch.UP does not automatically download programs, leaving that choice to the user.
Manageable Software Services which developed Catch.UP aims to provide accurate, dependable and timely updates for thousands of different software versions. Catch.UP is at www.manageable.com/
execute.html.
