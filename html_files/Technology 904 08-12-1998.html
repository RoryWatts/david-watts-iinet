Australia Federation centenary put online 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 08-12-1998

 ELEVEN national institutions are using the Web to explain and explore the centenary of Australia's Federation.
The National Council for the Centenary of Federation is coordinating the online project, called Windows on Federation.
The council's Windows Web site links to special centenary pages at: Australian National Maritime Museum; Australian War Memorial; High Court of Australia; National Archives; National Film and Sound Archive; National Gallery; National Library; National Museum; National Portrait Gallery; Old Parliament House and the Royal Australian Air Force.
The project's rich collection features songs, memorabilia, documents, important facts and images of the nation since Federation on January 1, 1901.
?Brought together through the Windows on Federation project, these items tell the story of our nation's beginnings more colourfully than most of the text books,? said the Minister for the Centenary of Federation, Peter McGauran.
Lenore Coltheart, research coordinator of public programs at the National Archives, said the centenary gave government agencies and cultural institutions a chance to work together creatively.
The Windows site is at: www.dcita.gov.au/windows/
