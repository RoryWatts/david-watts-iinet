Sports addicts, click here for quick fix 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 01-07-1997

 Sports lovers who can't get enough of their favourite game on TV will find plenty of almost live action of the big events on the Web.
Two of those - Wimbledon and the third cricket Test - usually hot up past most people's bedtime.
Be warned then: these constantly updated sites can become addictive and lead to snoring on the day job.
Coverage of the tennis at Wimbledon is near saturation. Web surfers can check games in progress. Scores are updated every five minutes and pictures are beamed from courtside.
Or they can check all results, the draw, players' biographies or Wimbledon's rich history. The truly committed can send e-mails to their favourite players.
The Third Test starts at Old Trafford on Thursday. The ABC is picking up the TV feed for this while Nine concentrates on the tennis.
But Aunty is not showing the early sessions. This means the cricket-starved can ?watch? the match on the CricInfo site, where scores are updated every few minutes. A ball-by-ball description is also offered as are scoreboards from nearly every other first-class game in the world.
AFL supporters can watch games fluctuate at the Melbourne Age's live footy site. Main games in each round are displayed on a Java scoreboard that updates with every goal and behind ? sometimes there are glitches.
Running match commentaries accompany the scores and a feedback forum enables fans to e-mail comments during a game. These comments are then thrown in with the commentary, giving a sense of virtual barracking.
