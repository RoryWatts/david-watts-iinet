Feathers ruffled as ugly chicken stays put 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 24-11-1997

 WE couch potatoes love our heroes. So last week when Peter Matera decided to stay with the Eagles I, like many one-eyed West Coast followers, could barely contain my joy.
Straightaway I sent a fax to my mate Bill. It had a picture of Matera and the words ?Bill, you know I would never leave you, love Peter?.
Like me, Bill thinks the sun shines out of Matera's you-know-where.
If the champion Eagle had done nothing more after kicking that goal on the run from the boundary in the 1992 grand final, he would still have been a superstar.
But of course he has done plenty. Often, it seems Matera is the only reason the Eagles remain competitive.
His dash and evasion from the back pocket have helped change the course of many games.
At the night game against Carlton at Subiaco this year I sat near a man who, though decked out in full Eagles clobber, could easily have passed for a Blues barracker.
You know the type. Everything West Coast did was met with derision. ?Get some skills Bluey. You've lost it Woosha - ahhh Drew, you're not good enough. Keep on walking Chad, right off the field.?
He didn't even join the mandatory booing of Greg Williams, so I thought he might be an enemy plant.
However, when Matera lifted - as he nearly always does - this joker yelled: ?Does this bloke have to do everything? I don't know why he bothers sometimes.?
Thus he revealed himself a true Eagle - not your textbook flag-waver, but one who knew a champion at least. Not every football follower shares this near reverence for Matera, though.
More than a few hurtful arrows have been fired in various forums on the Internet.
The Eagles have their own site on the World Wide Web with a discussion area that allows anyone, even Collingwood and Hawthorn fans, to put in their 20 cents worth in the form of e-mail messages.
One appeared under the heading ?Matera is garbage? and said: ?I don't know why one team let alone two would want that uncoordinated ugly chicken in their side.? It was posted by someone using the nickname Collingwood Boy.
It provoked immediate replies, including this: ?For your information, Peter Matera is a skilled player and better than any of the excuses for players that Collingwood has produced. I bet that if Peter Matera played for the Magpies then you would love him too. Oh, and by the way BEN COUSINS RULES.?
Elsewhere, in a discussion group devoted to football and which has the air of a pub in a constant state of post-match debate, the electronic conversation went like this: ?Peter Matera will be staying with the Eagles for at least the next four years.
?Melbourne players should expect a decent pay rise now that there's plenty of room left below the salary cap.?
One response: ?Funny, I always thought that you had to play well to get a pay rise.?
There is no accounting for taste.
