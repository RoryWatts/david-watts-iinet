Categories set terms for access 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 11-02-1997

 THE Recreational Software Advisory Council uses five levels to label World Wide Web sites. Sites are graded 0 to 4 with 0 representing unlimited access.
A user can set software like CyberPatrol or the Microsoft Internet Explorer 3.0 browser to any of these levels and material rated at that level or higher will be blocked.
The categories are:
Violence Level 0: Harmless conflict; some damage to objects. Level 1: Creatures injured or killed; damage to objects. Level 2: Humans injured or killed; with small amount of blood. Level 3: Humans injured or killed; blood and gore. Level 4: Wanton and gratuitous violence; torture; rape.
Nudity/sex Level 0: No nudity or revealing attire; romance, no sex. Level 1: Revealing attire; passionate kissing. Level 2: Partial nudity ; clothed sexual touching. Level 3: Non-sexual frontal nudity; non-explicit sexual activity. Level 4: Provocative frontal nudity; explicit sexual activity; sex crimes
Language Level 0: Inoffensive slang; no profanity. Level 1: Mild expletives. Level 2: Expletives; non-sexual anatomical references. Level 3: Strong, vulgar language; obscene gestures. Level 4: Crude or explicit sexual references.
Each level can be broken down further for more thorough filtering.
For example, Level 1 violence can stipulate not to show sites where creatures are injured or there is damage to realistic objects.
Level 2 nudity/sex can ban partial nudity sites but allow passionate kissing, or allow partial nudity and block sites which show ?clothed sexual touching?.
