Date: 22-06-1999

THE ExploreZip worm is wrecking Windows systems around the world for one reason - Internet users continue to ignore advice and open attachments in unsolicited e-mail.
This page from Ziff Davis Net guru Jesse Berst will tell people all about the latest virus scare and give them sound all-round advice about protecting their systems.
Berst says millions of users are at risk: ?If you're among them, be very careful with e-mail. Do not open e-mail with attachments unless you're certain the sender is safe, and only after you've downloaded protective software.?
The ExploreZip worm - first discovered in Israel - enters Windows 95, 98 and NT systems as an e-mail attachment with the name ?zipped-files.exe?.
If it executes, the worm copies itself to the Windows system directory with the filename ?Explore.exe? and then modifies the WIN.INI file so the program executes each time Windows starts.
It then uses the infected computer's e-mail client to harvest e-mail addresses so it can propagate itself. Behind the scenes, it searches through C to Z drives and selects crucial file extensions, rendering them useless by making them 0 bytes long.
ANTI-CENSORSHIP
Prairie Dog
www.prairie-dog.net
PERTH woman Bernadette Taylor recently hit the headlines by claiming that government workers around Australia had been using taxpayer-funded computer time to leer at the Web-cam pictures she posts of herself at her Virtual Girlfriend site.
Now Ms Taylor has beefed up her Web presence with this site protesting against the Federal Government's Net censorship legislation.
It includes a long list of the government addresses she says have been looking at her intimate pictures. But its main feature is a well-constructed analysis of the legislation and her opinion on why it will not work.
