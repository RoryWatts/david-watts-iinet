Learn to speak geek 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 05-08-1997

 Feeling confused? The language of computing is daunting for new users.
What is a gopher, or WAIS, or MIME, for instance? Can you cope with POP, HTML, SMTP and WYSIWYG?
Happily, the Web has the answers to an incredible range of subjects. Some sites claim to know the meaning of life.
Technical explanations abound for new and allegedly experienced users alike.
One of the better sources is the TechWeb Technology Encyclopedia. Rather than offer pages and pages of book-like alphabetical listings like a traditional encyclopaedia or dictionary, TechWeb offers a search engine.
Simply key in the technical term or phrase you need to know, and the encyclopaedia provides either a simple sentence or a detailed chapter, depending on the type of query. For instance, tap in ?World Wide Web? and you are given a comprehensive history of its development with cross-references to subjects such as HTML and the history of the Net itself.
The other factor which sets TechWeb apart from other technical tools is the ability - for Windows95 users at least - to download a demonstration copy of the multimedia version of the database.
Weighing in at only 660Kb, the demo enables users to set up their own technical encyclopaedia on the desktop.
Being a demo, it is somewhat crippled. It has no pictures, for example, and several categories are missing. But it offers a taste of what a $40 CD version of the software might bring.
Elsewhere, simple and effective translation of technical jargon is available at Netdictionary and Whatis.
Both are more traditional dictionary sites but offer quick references and a reasonable amount of humour, including geek-speak.
