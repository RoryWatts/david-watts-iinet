Life and sport go on amid tragedy 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 02-09-1997

 CHANNEL 7 was right to stick with the football last Sunday afternoon, despite the passing of the Princess of Wales.
Nine showed blanket CNN and Sky coverage while the ABC relayed the BBC's sombre broadcasts, which included the chilling announcement of Diana's death to a goosebumpy backdrop of the Union Jack at half mast while God Save the Queen played.
But life goes on even amid great tragedy. And Seven acknowledged it by giving viewers the lift of a thrilling last round.
If there is a lesson to be learnt in both sport and life, it is to expect the unexpected and to prepare for as many downs as ups.
The sporting roller-coaster had plenty of each at the weekend. Let's examine them:
Up - Jeff Farmer's virtuoso performance for wooden spooner Melbourne at the MCG.
Down - It was against the Dockers, who should now be allowed to lick their wounds in peace, knowing they are still at least a year away from serious finals contention.
Up - The spirited games of Richmond, Essendon and Collingwood. None had anything to play for but pride, and their wins over Carlton, Adelaide and North were great advertisements for the game.
Down - The fact that poor, jaded Hawthorn could not join them by knocking off the beastly Western Bulldogs to ensure a final in Perth for West Coast.
Up - The stirring early efforts of Port Adelaide against St Kilda as it tried to sneak a finals spot.
Down - It was a vain bid and was shown on TV way past most people's bedtime.
Up - The record crowd of 46,000 at the SCG on Saturday night.
Down - They saw the flaws of their beloved Swans exposed by a very good Geelong side.
Up - The strength and skills of West Coast against Brisbane.
Down - They were apparent only for a quarter and a half. The Eagles now have to trot their stuff at Football Park in front of more than 40,000 Crows supporters. There is room for only 2000 opposition fans.
Up - Brisbane made the eight.
Down - Brisbane made the eight.
The ups and downs were not restricted to the AFL, of course. For example:
Up - The powerful form of Pat Rafter in the US Open tennis championships.
Down - The Channel 9 telecasts start at 1am which is basically no one's fault because of the time difference, but a highlights package at a reasonable hour might help.
Up - The action-packed highlights of the world cycling championships on SBS.
Down - The crash of the Ukrainian pursuit team when it had gold in its grasp.
