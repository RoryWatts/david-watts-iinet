WA users set the mark in inventive footy sites 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 16-04-1996

 WA INTERNET users are on top of the ladder in creating inventive football sites on the World Wide Web.
Outmarking all is Todd Culley of Curtin University whose homage to Geelong superstar Gary Ablett has the ability to stop surfers in their tracks.
We see Gary Ablett leaping to take the mark of the year against Collingwood in round seven, 1994.
A skilful overlay of pictures is repeated for as long as the surfer stays with the page, letting Ablett fly repeatedly. In the great Jack Dyer's words: ?He jumps so high he marks a seagull.?
As well as the animated ?speccy? there are details of Ablett's career, achievements, more pictures and Geelong results, regularly updated.
Chemical engineering graduate Andrew Dare has jumped into The West Australian's Topteam fervour.
At his impressive WAFL site, Mr Dare allows users to download a Topteam program which he wrote that will allow football enthusiasts to make team selections and chart their progress.
Topteam is written for Excel and is simple to follow, mapping all players named in The West Australian by position and ranking.
The WAFL site has been one of the best football sites in Australia and gives a wide array of information about WA's own league.
The West Coast Eagles team has its own official home page now and it will gradually ease out the W'Eagle Web started by supporters last year.
Eagles spokesman Peter Taliangis said last week the official page was launched a fortnight ago after weeks of testing.
West Coast Web has everything W'Eagle Web had and has added new features. In fact Karen McCulloch, who prepared W'Eagle Web, is the major creator of West Coast Web.
The Fremantle Dockers had an unofficial site all last year, but it has been dismantled. A handsome official site has now been established.
