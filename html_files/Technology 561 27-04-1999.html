Nudegirl plays the Net shuffle game 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 27-04-1999

 THIS is an example of how Australia's Net censorship might work after January 1, 2000:
1: The creator of a Web site - defined by the legislation as a content host - publishes a page containing pictures of naked supermodels. He calls the page www.nudegirl.com.au.
2: A Net user is offended by the photos on the page and complains in writing to the Australian Broadcasting Authority.
3: The ABA first has to decide whether the complaint is frivolous, in which case it will go no further. In this case it decides www.nudegirl.com.au needs investigation.
4: The nudegirl creator is issued with a formal notice from the ABA to cease publication of the site. If he does not comply he can be fined on a daily basis.
5: The ABA passes the page to the National Classification Board which will determine what classification to give it. (The board is currently responsible for rating other media).
6: The classification board gives www.nudegirl.com.au an R-rating and tells the creator the site can be uploaded to an ISP which has age-verification software in a bid to stop under-18s accessing it.
7: www.nudegirl.com.au is posted on the Web again, but the ISP has not been able to provide the age-checking software.
8: The ABA issues a formal notice to the ISP telling it to take www.nudegirl.com.au off the Web or face daily fines until it does.
The legislation will not apply to Web content only. Wording of the Bill suggests that newsgroups and chat sites can also be the subject of complaints.
Senator Alston says the legislation will not cover private communications such as intraNets, so it is unlikely e-mail will fall under the ABA's jurisdiction.
Critics say that in the above example, the creator of www.nudegirl.com.au could simply buy or find a free site for his content and change its name to something like www.girl.com.au.
The content that had run through the bureaucratic chain would then be freely available, at any time, until another written complaint was made about it.
