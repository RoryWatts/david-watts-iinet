More than meets eye 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 17-06-1997

 Superman actor Christopher Reeve is truly a man of steel.
The 44-year-old was cut down in a tragic riding accident two years ago. He is crippled and wheelchair-bound.
But he works non-stop, not only back in his old field of film-making, but most prominently as a strong, clear voice for others in the world immobilised by paralysis.
Reeve's fight is now on the Web at the Circle of Friends, a strikingly designed site which is part of the American Paralysis Association's campaign to find a cure for spinal cord injury.
Reeve's striking face is central to the thrust of the opening page of the site, which boasts a unique design feature - it is about 3000 pixels wide. Most computer screens are only 800 wide, so you do not get the whole story unless you use the bottom scroll bar.
The aims of the site and the support of a galaxy of Hollywood stars, including Tom Hanks and Mel Gibson, are then spelt out.
The method in this apparent madness is to give surfers a feeling of the perspective endured by paralysis victims.
?People with spinal cord injuries spend the day in a wheelchair. Their perspective is confined to a horizontal plane,? the site explains.
?This home page scrolls on a horizontal plane as a subtle reminder of how people with spinal cord injury live everyday.
?This home page will remain horizontal until research finds a cure and people with spinal cord injury can rise to the occasion.?
