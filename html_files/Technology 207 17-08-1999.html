Cockburn city site is a Web winner 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 17-08-1999

 COMMUNITY
City of Cockburn
www.cockburn.wa.gov.au
THE City of Cockburn has made its mark on the world ... not quite, but its presence on the World Wide Web is an eye-opener.
The attractive use of images, digital manipulation and music has won the city this year's best Web site prize in the WA Municipal Association's Effective Communications Awards.
Glenn Hankinson, of Osborne Park company Dow Digital, who helped the council put its site together, said Cockburn now had a well designed, attractive and informative medium for ratepayers.
Mr Hankinson praised his Dow team and the council for the use of dynamic technologies such as Flash and Audio on the site.
This gave it a vibrant and colourful presence, he said.
The highlight is the Flash welcome page.
This gives way to an easily navigated and fast conventional site full of information about the Cockburn area and council news and services.
Pages for council contacts and e-mail feedback are also provided.
MUSIC SOFTWARE
Real Jukebox new beta 2.0
www.real.com
REAL Networks released its first version of Jukebox three months ago to critical acclaim.
This updated version is even better and certainly more stable.
Real Jukebox is one of the more innovative bits of software to come online in recent times.
It allows users to record CDs straight to their hard drives and to manage collections of the music in ways that best suit the user.
Jukebox also probes the Web for information about CDs and can play dozens of different types of formats, including the ever present MP3.
And above all, it is free in its basic version which is only a 7Mb download.
FUN
I Resign
www.i-resign.com
YOU could call this site a homage to quit wit. I Resign puts the spotlight on that vital part of corporate culture ... the resignation letter.
It provides samples for those wanting a graceful exit from their jobs, although many of them are more likely to result in the withholding of severance pay, if not immediate attention from company security guards.
The important thing is not to take any of it too seriously.
And users are asked to submit their resignation letters as well to broaden the human experience.
Letters can be serious, but it is probably better if they aren't.
Here is an excerpt from the site's example of a gloating resignation letter . .. and it is said to be genuine: ?I've decided not to take up your offer of more money and a new Peugeot.
?Actually, I'd rather like to spit on your offer of more money and a new Peugeot.
?What sort of idiot do you think I am to even consider accepting your trifling gesture, this pittance, this worst of kind of tokenism? I'm off; outta here ...?
