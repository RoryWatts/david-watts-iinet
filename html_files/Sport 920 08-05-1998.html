East is least and West is . . . 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 08-05-1998

 I HAVEN'T been feeling that well this week. So I went to a psychiatrist. Here is what transpired:
Psychiatrist (hereinafter referred to as P): Now tell me, what seems to be the problem?
Me (hereinafter referred to as Me): I think I'm becoming a West Aussie.
P: Now what could be the matter with that?
Me: Well you see I'm a Queenslander born and bred and proud of it . . . or so I thought.
P: Being a Queenslander is a serious illness you know.
Me: Yes but I've learnt to live with it or at least I thought I had.
P: That is good. Overcoming the Hanson/Bjelke-Petersen syndrome is difficult. But what makes you think it's all changed and you're a Sandgroper?
Me: Well I really believe Justin Langer is the victim of an eastern States conspiracy and should be in the Test team ahead of Elliott, Bevan or even Mark Waugh.
P: Ummm. Queenslanders are prone to conspiracy theories like that, too. Think of Peter Burge and Sam Trimble in the 60s. It might simply be displacement.
Me: But I think Brendon Julian should be there, too.
P: Still doesn't prove anything. Let's try a bit of word association. When I say a word, you respond with the first thing that comes to mind.
Sardines, Freo
Cappuccino, Freo
Exciting football, Freo
Fine wine, Margaret River.
Great sportsmen Dennis Lillee, Herb Elliott, Margaret Court, Polly Farmer, Shirley Strickland, Neil Brooks, Barry Cable . . .
P: That's enough. Very interesting. Name some of the greats of Australian showbusiness.
Me: Johnny Young, Baby John Burgess, Gary Carvolth, Lionel York . . .
P: Enough, enough. This is looking potentially serious. Let's try authors.
Me: Tim Winton, Elizabeth Jolley, David Malouf.
P: Ah, but Malouf is a Queenslander isn't he?
Me: But he writes like a West Australian.
Y
P: Okay, calm down. How about food? Play the word game again.
Fish, herring, caught off the jetty at Rottnest.
Fruit, apples from Donnybrook. Bananas from Carnarvon.
P: Tsk, tsk, a Banana-Bender too, we may be on to something.
Beef, prime Harvey fillet, grain-fed and tenderised, best accompanied by a peppery cab sav from the South-West or Pemberton if you're adventurous.
P: Well that might just prove you're reading too many menus or that bloke Jordan's wine column. Let's try some different associations.
Surf, Prevelly Park.
Holiday, Rotto.
Coastline, between Denmark and Walpole.
P: Have you been anywhere or seen anything else? What are you comparing these places with?
Me: I've been to New York, Paris, Rome, Dublin, Boston, Hawaii, all round Australia and I've lived in London.
P: What did you think while you were there?
Me: That I'd much rather be on a veranda facing an Indian Ocean sunset, which turns the light green grass a straw colour, while sipping a chardonnay, which is also a straw colour . . .
P: Dear me. You are exhibiting an awful lot of symptoms. What do you think finally alerted you to this West Australianness?
Me: At the WACA a couple of weeks ago I was watching the Eagles v Sydney. A great game, nip and tuck all the way. O'Loughlin from Sydney kicked a lovely goal that put them 20 points ahead with 10 minutes left.
P: So?
Me: I spat the dummy. I left the ground. We were going to lose. I couldn't bear it.
P: There, there. I don't want to push you as it's your first time on the couch. But how did you feel after Sunday's two-point loss to St Kilda?
Me: Loss, loss. That was never a loss. &%@** umpires robbed us. It was a moral victory I tell you. I agree with George. Those Easterners are out to get us.Richard Court is the greatest politician in the land and Eric Charlton is a genius. Tow icebergs to the Pilbara, build a great wall from Eucla to Kununurra. To arms, stop the Easterners. Secede . . .
