Canberra likely to pursue Net curbs 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 18-05-1999

 THE Federal Government is likely to push ahead with Internet censorship legislation, despite Senator Brian Harradine's opposition to the goods and services tax.
Critics of the legislation have claimed that the proposed censorship was an attempt to win over Senator Harradine, a strong morals crusader who has been openly contemptuous of salacious Net content.
But a spokesman for Communications Minister Richard Alston yesterday dismissed talk of the legislation being sidelined.
The Bill, intended to set up a regime to block and filter X and R-rated content, would be debated in the Senate as soon as possible.
A Senate committee approved the legislation last week after the public had been given only nine days to make submissions.
More than 100 submissions claimed that the legislation would make Australian Net access slower and more expensive, would cripple e-commerce, fail to make a dent in offensive content and make Australia a laughing stock.
Leading opponents of the legislation say the Bill is a product of politicians' ignorance of the Net.
