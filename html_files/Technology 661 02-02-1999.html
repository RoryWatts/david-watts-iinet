Survey finds 41pc online in US 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 02-02-1999

 THE Internet user population continues to become more mainstream, according to a recent US survey.
The Pew Research Centre for the People and the Press found that 41 per cent of Americans were online, compared with 23 per cent two years ago.
The increase in the number of users who were non-professionals, women or without tertiary education had led to a significant change in the type of content sought.
Two years ago technology news was the top attraction. But now the weather was the most popular online news attraction.
Pew, which interviewed more than 3100 people, said its survey showed that 42 per cent of US users looked for local news, up from 27 per cent in 1996.
One in five people now used the Net regularly to get up-to-date news and weather reports.
Pew concluded that the Net may be replacing television as a source of news. Heavy Internet users read newspapers and listened to the radio as much as the rest of the population, but watched TV much less.
A substantial minority of Internet users - 44 per cent - said they thought the Net news sites were more accurate than daily newspapers or television news programs.
Those using the Net for news cited three reasons: the ability to get information unavailable elsewhere, its convenience, and the ability to search for specific subjects.
?The popularity of general interest subjects online - from weather patterns to entertainment news - is growing much faster than that of political or international news,? Pew found.
An online summary of the survey claimed that the findings about an increasingly mainstream user population did not appear to reflect a trend towards social isolation.
?The broadening of the Internet population also casts doubt on suggestions that online activities are associated with interpersonal distrust and that the Internet attracts malcontents,? the summary said.
?Frequent Internet users are just as trusting of other people and the government as the average American, even when factors like age, education and income are taken into account.?
