Rivals work on upgrades to Net guides 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 07-07-1998

 THE browser war may have changed battlegrounds to business-oriented Web marketing, or portals, but both Netscape and Microsoft are continuing to upgrade their products.
Within days Netscape will release the beta version of the Communicator 4.5, and Microsoft already has made a developer version of Internet Explorer 5.0 available on the Web.
Communicator 4.5 will feature major product enhancements including Smart Browsing, high performance Internet messaging through open standards such as LDAP and IMAP, and Roaming Access support for mobile users.
Smart Browsing is designed to allow users to find information quickly and easily by seamlessly integrating a new generation of services, hosted by the Netscape Netcenter portal site, with Netscape Communicator 4.5 client software.
"Smart Browsing is designed to allow users to find information quickly and easily. '
Smart Browsing has three main components. Internet Keywords will allow users to access Web sites by typing regular words, or simple ?keywords?, instead of specific Internet addresses.
What's Related will provide users with a dynamically generated list of links to information about companies, services and products that relate to the site that the user is visiting.
NetWatch will provide a mechanism for screening Internet content based on two PICS-compliant rating systems, RSACi and SafeSurf.
Web commentator Jesse Berst of ZDNet has hailed Netscape for pioneering browsing concepts with 4.5.
On the other hand, he recently labelled IE 5.0 as further evidence - along with Windows 98 - of Microsoft concentrating on the software engineering side of its new programs.
?Most of its improvements will appeal largely to developers who build on top of IE,? Mr Berst said.
