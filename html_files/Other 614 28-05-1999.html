May the Force be with you in croquet 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 28-05-1999

 I ONCE played croquet against C-3PO and he beat me comfortably. He was not wearing the gold-coloured robot suit that sets him apart as the prissy translator in the Star Wars movies. But I knew it was him. He told me so.
My croquet conqueror actually introduced himself as Anthony Daniels and with a hang-dog look and morose voice, so different to the fussy high tones in the films, proceeded to answer my questions about being a famous robot.
He was sick of being C-3PO. All people wanted him to do was to don his gold suit and be fussy and pedantic. Big Japanese shopping centres could not get enough of him.
He had even conducted the London Symphony Orchestra in front of a packed house at London's Royal Albert Hall . . . in his fibreglass C-3PO costume.
He hated R2D2. His ambition to be a serious stage actor was in tatters.
Truly, Daniels had paid the price of fame. When I think about it he is the most famous person I have met, certainly the most famous - in fact the only person - I have played croquet against.
I met him - Daniels and C-3PO - in 1990, on a summer's day at a sumptuous country house in Sussex.
Why was I there? Why was C-3PO beside me, failing miserably, like all Poms, to get lunch together on the barbecue?
My wife had met another GYM (Gorgeous Young Mum) at a gorgeous young mums-to-be meeting and they had become friends. This other GYM was a costume designer whose partner was a budding producer. They had been invited to the Sussex weekend retreat of the business editor of a London newspaper. He was the producer's mentor and sponsor and would look at his latest plans.
The GYM and the producer asked my wife and me along. When we got to the pretty house and its rolling lawns - perfect for croquet - the first thing I noticed was that the business editor's company car was a Porsche. Mine - I was something to do with computers at a rival paper - was a poky little Peugeot.
Clearly, our different newspapers were on different financial planets.
The second thing I noticed was this sad, thin man, who turned out to be the famous C-3PO.
Why bring all this hob-nobbing and croquet up now?
Well, Anthony Daniels, whose career before Star Wars involved much Shakespearean work with the Young Vic, is back as C-3PO in the much-hyped Star Wars: Episode 1 - The Phantom Menace, which hits Perth on Thursday.
Actually Daniels will not be in C-3PO's suit because as the film is set long before the three we have already seen, C-3PO is in an early prototype stage. He has no innards, so Daniels will supply only the well-known voice.
Despite his protestations while belting me at croquet, Daniels really has a soft spot for his character. He has revealed his liking at one of the many Internet sites devoted to C-3PO. ?See-Threepio (as real Star Wars fanatics call him) is really a very loveable character, which is one reason why I stuck with him,? says Daniels.
"He has the manners and qualities of a well-bred, well-paid, high-class English butler. He is very much at home in any social gathering where cocktails are being served and people are behaving politely to one another (croquet games, for example).?
Daniels appears to have come to terms with his lot. Since Return of the Jedi was made 16 years ago, C-3PO has been trotted out in films called Special Effects: Anything Can Happen, Star Tours and a TV special called Droids (The Adventures of C-3PO and R2D2).
Away from life as C-3PO, Daniels has managed parts in two Prime Suspect episodes and a film titled I Bought a Vampire Motorcycle.
I intend to have a look at the Phantom Menace once the over-hyped crowds start to thin.
You might be lucky enough to sit beside me. I'll be the one cheering when C-3PO appears and saying: ?Did you know he once beat me at croquet??
