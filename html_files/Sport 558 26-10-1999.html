Twice the fun with Handy and company 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 26-10-1999

 I WEAR an eye-patch because of double vision.
When I take it off I see see things things twice twice.
I took it off just then to demonstrate what I mean. It's safely back on now, so no need to worry.
But I must admit I took it off on Saturday night to watch the rugby union World Cup quarter final between Wales and Australia from Cardiff.
Thus I was able to see it twice, once on Foxtel and once on Channel 7.
Foxtel showed the Wallaby victory live and had commercials only at half-time.
The commentary was from an Englishman and a Welshman and was steady and relatively unemotional - except when referee Colin Hawke missed Tim Horan's blatant knock-on that led to George Gregan scoring Australia's third try and made the score a very flattering 24-9.
Everyone got emotional about that. Perhaps Mr Hawke was wearing an eye-patch or two.
Foxtel's coverage was so unbiased, smooth and sane that I switched to Seven when Ben Tune body-surfed in for a try after following up a clever, skidding kick from Stephen Larkham mid-way through the second half.
And what joys Seven had. It was just showing the teams lining up for the national anthems at the start and was panning across the boys from Wales.
It was not a pretty sight. Most of the forwards would make West Coast's Scott Cummings look like a skinny wretch. Axed Western Bulldog James Cook could happily put on 15kg if he wanted to switch codes.
The sight of the Welsh supported the AAPT Smart Chat commercial which features former Wallaby great Mark Ella and a bloke who is supposed to know nothing about the game.
?Have you ever seen Wales play?? Ella asks the bloke.?No I haven't,? he replies. ?Wales are animals,? says Ella. ?Yes I know. A lot of people think they are fish,? counters the bloke.
Seven then showed the Australians. My wife and children fled the room in horror.
It got on with the game (which was nearly finished on Foxtel) and suddenly the first half became much more exciting and dramatic than the one Foxtel had shown.
Funny that, because both used exactly the same vision and it was exactly the same defence-dominated scrap in the rain.
But Gordon Bray, Chris Handy and Simon Poidevin are all excellent graduates from the McAvaney-Roberts school of drama, and they were barracking for the Wallabies a fair bit of the time - they probably wear eye-patches, too.
Now I like rugby. I know that a lot of West Australians are baffled by it. They think it's about a lot of boofy blokes diving on the ball and the referee breaking it up and awarding a penalty for an unfathomable reason.
Whereas we who have played it actually know for certain it's about a lot of boofy blokes diving on the ball and the referee breaking it up and awarding a penalty for an unfathomable reason.
