Kilobytes catch up with Star Trek Net generation 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 03-09-1996

 THE definition of what is cool on the Web changes constantly as more people join in and the profile of the Net population alters.
Where Pamela Anderson pages or Star Trek homages drew most hits and adulation only two years ago, the quest for artistic and literary quality now appears to have more merit.
Netizens are getting older, there are a growing number of women, and businesses now see the Net as a useful tool.
Still there are certain criteria that set one site apart from another. The staff at InfiNet have listed five hints for creating cool sites.
And as curators of Cool Site of the Day and Cool Site of the Year they should know.
First they advise surfers to create something useful.
?On a bad Internet day downloading a Web page is like watching the grass grow - only with advertisements,? InfiNet says.
?We don't expect your page to cure psoriasis, but you'll have a better chance at being Cool Site of the Day if you provide goodies for your visitors.?
InfiNet suggests images of leading celebrities, a search engine or even carefully thought-out personal opinions on a topical subject.
But if surfers cannot be useful they can be entertaining, InfiNet says.
The staff say they value this trait ?because it explains why we bought Kiss albums when we were teenagers?.
Size and download times are also important. Every kilobyte on a page takes roughly a second to download meaning it can take up to two minutes to see a 100kb page.
InfiNet tells surfers with an addiction to big files to try to use their time more productively ... ?like going out in the real world and dating?.
Personal pictures are frowned on: ?Unless 100 people have told you that you're beautiful or remarkable, don't put your photo on a Web page.?
The exceptions are those surfers who have third eyes, extra limbs or odd proportions.
The final piece of InfiNet advice on cool sites is probably the most invaluable ... don't forget to submit the URL.
The staff say they have had dozens of descriptions of what sound truly cool pages, only to find there is no Web address ... which is uncool.
