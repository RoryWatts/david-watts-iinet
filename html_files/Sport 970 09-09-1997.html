Aussies show their colours 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 09-09-1997

 IMAGINE Mick Malthouse commentating on the Adelaide-West Coast final on Sunday.
It sounds bizarre, even though the intense Eagles coach is capable of some of the better, if more enigmatic, lines on the game.
Remember ?The ox is slow but the earth is patient?? On Sunday, he had: ?The most obvious (thing that went wrong) was losing the game.?
Well, TV viewers had a rough parallel with a Malthouse commentary during the US Open tennis final served as a sporting feast by Channel 9 around breakfast.
Queensland-born Bermuda resident Pat Rafter beat Montreal-born Englishman Greg Rusedski in a brilliant match. Observing from the commentary box for Nine was Rafter's Davis Cup captain and Australian tennis legend John Newcombe.
Newc could not resist coaching his powerful charge, particularly as he fought to overcome Rusedski's impressive counter-attack in what turned out to be the final set.
There was no evidence that the tide was turning either way, the contest was so even. But in the box, Newc, and to a lesser extent his Australian colleague with the growing American accent Fred Stolle (?all you folks back home?), started some very one-sided barracking.
Newcombe: We need a low backhand volley (Rafter obliges).
Newcombe: Yes!
Stolle: All right. Just relax now, just relax.
Newcombe: I will if he gets the ball low down at his feet.
Stolle: Remember it's only a game, Newc (chuckles to himself).
Newcombe proceeded in similar vein to get his boy to match point. He threw in several emphatic yeses, a well done and a fatherly, ?Okay, don't overplay it now.?
On the big point, he advised: ?Wide serve, then backhand cross-court volley.?
Rafter did not follow the script, preferring to go straight at Rusedski. He won anyway.
Then followed some wonderful moments of exhilaration as he performed effortlessly the Pat Cash climb into the crowd to hug his support team.
And that attracted some wry comments from Newc, too. ?Not too much hugging of Rochie (his coach Tony Roche). He doesn't like that.?
To cap it off, Newc interviewed the overjoyed champion and signed off with the ominous threat that he would give him a kiss when the cameras went off.
All in all it was parochial, one-sided and biased.
But it was also great fun and right in the slot if you were a passionate supporter of Australian tennis wanting a slice of triumph after a 10-year drought.
The question of having one-sided commentators is a vexed one. Newcombe can carry it off because, for a start, he is a much-admired Australian champion.
He also has a nice sense of humour and offers good insights into the game and players.
Tennis on this scale can also afford this sort of bias because it is a contest between individuals, and despite Rafter's win, one in which Australians often need all the support they can get.
If Rafter had lost it might have been a different proposition.
For the moment the old saying applies: ?Winners are grinners.?
