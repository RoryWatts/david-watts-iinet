Say sorry on the Internet 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 09-06-1997

 A PERTH Internet site is offering people the chance to apologise personally to the stolen generation of Aborigines forcefully separated from their families.
Federal Government inaction and frustration with Prime Minister John Howard's muted personal apology prompted Perth man Anthony Shipley to create the site on the World Wide Web.
The site, Apology Australia, invites people to leave their name and e-mail address. A list of surnames can then be displayed, representing not only individual apologies but a collective one as well.
Mr Shipley hopes the site will attract many cyber-signatures. He would like it to remain on the Web as long as possible ?as a memorial to the suffering of the stolen generation so that this should never happen again?.
He believes that refusing to apologise for the trauma of having children taken away or of being separated from parents is uncharitable.
?The site is not about compensation,? he said. ?Largely, I want to focus on the apology without muddying the waters with party politics or the question of compensation.
?The stolen generation deserve an apology from the Australian people. I don't want to do anything that would put that at risk.
The Web address of Apology Australia is: http://apology.west.net.au/
