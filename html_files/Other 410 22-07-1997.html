Worldwide weather watch 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 22-07-1997

 People talk about the weather all the time. The Web gives them the chance to be better informed in their small talk.
The Perth Bureau of Meteorology, like its counterparts in other States, lays claim to one of the least glamorous Web sites of all. It is an old-fashioned gopher site. Gopher is a software tool predating the Web which enables you to search for files using a text-based interface.
The Perth site is packed withup-to-date information.
The official forecasts, climate trends and satellite maps available at the site can make the user as well informed as the ABC's Michael Schultz or Ten's John Barnett.
Travellers or those who just have to know the forecast for Test cricket venues, for example, have a wide choice of global weather pages.
Yahoo is as comprehensive with weather as it is with every other subject it touches.
Its forecasts, from Weathernews Inc, are presented with neat graphics predicting clouds, showers, rain, snow and sunshine.
Users can also see satellite pictures from dozens of locations, or download short MPEG movies taken from the weather satellites.
Intellicast offers the most graphic forecasts. They are not always as up to date as they might be, however.
The US site is predictably America-oriented, but its world section has an attractive and easily followed format of clickable maps.
