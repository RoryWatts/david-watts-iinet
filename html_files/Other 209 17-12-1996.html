Dilbert's site offers a daily mind workout 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 17-12-1996

 DILBERT is a favourite among Net users, judging by the number of links and references to the irreverent comic strip from home pages on the World Wide Web.
The bespectacled character and his office workmates, who appear daily in The West Australian , have a new site which is winning plaudits.
Angela Gunn of Yahoo's Y-Life says she is an unashamed devotee of Dilbert and the fun it pokes at the eccentricities and often sheer boredom of office life.
She writes that one of the best parts of the new Dilbert Zone site is the Daily Mental Workout .
?For those of us not yet entirely resigned to office brain-death, there's a daily dose of pain from the Mensa Genius Quiz-A-Day Book; for the managerial side, there's a weekly version that's not from Mensa. We don't want any heads exploding,? Ms Gunn says.
She rates Dilbert as the definitive cartoon strip of the second half of the 1990s.
Doonesbury, she says, was the strip for the early 1970s; Garfield in the late 1970s; and Bloom County and Calvin and Hobbes split the 1980s and the first part of the 1990s.
