Hollywood driveways a goldmine 

Author: DAVID WATTS; DAVID WATTS

Date: 10-06-1997

 The Internet may be replacing television as a home entertainment medium but the idiot box still supplies invaluable material for the World Wide Web.
One of those shows that could happen only in America is called Driveways of the Rich and Famous, which has had a strong cult following on cable TV for four years.
It also has its own Web site as a spin-off. And it is an amusing, entertaining and offbeat one, especially for those who do not take Tinsel Town glitter too seriously.
John Cunningham, mastermind of Driveways both on TV and the Web, takes a small video camera to prominent people's places and photographs their driveways.
Every now and then he bumps into the actual celebrity using their drive. Mostly he makes do interviewing delivery men, pool maintenance people, garbos and postmen.
The results are often fascinating. For example, he was stopped by security from venturing down Playboy boss Hugh Hefner's driveway. Security happened to be a voice coming from a big rock.
Cunningham interviewed the rock and got some candid insights.
He also found singer Trini Lopez washing his car and admitting he was a devoted fan.
Madonna was less kind but at least he got photos of her wheeling out of her drive on her bike.
The Driveways site features transcripts of many of the show's interviews plus dozens of stills of celebrity driveways.
If you have a hankering to see shots of drives leading to the homes of Andre Agassi, Marlon Brando, Geena Davis, Harrison Ford, Julia Roberts or many more, look no further.
