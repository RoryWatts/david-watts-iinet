When you're 45, sock it to "em 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 06-08-1999

 I TURNED 45 recently. Can't remember exactly when. That is one of the benefits of middle-aged foggiedom . . . you don't have to worry about precise ages, or whatever it is I was talking about.
There are many benefits to being 45. For a start, when young men yell ?you're a bald, fat, old git? at you as you walk down the street, you can just look them in the eyes and smile.
This is because not only are you a bald, fat, old git; it is because you are also a deaf, bald, fat, old git.
When a teenager comes up and asks what you think of her latest Green Day CD, you can hold her gaze and exclaim: ?Sorry I missed that because of the noise of that junk coming from the stereo. Put on this Bee Gees album, would you love? And turn the volume down a bit.?
If you go to a gymnasium or a swimming pool, you don't have to pretend you are there to improve your physique. Everyone there will agree with you that it is many kilos and wrinkles beyond hope.
And they will know the truth - you have just come to look at the gorgeous young women in their lycra.
If the song running around in your head goes ?Monday night, we're Talking Footy?, you won't have to be the tiniest bit embarrassed. For a start, you know that no one will ever ask because they couldn't care less.
Even if they do, they probably wouldn't be at all surprised.
On weekends, when everyone else under 45 is out having a real life, you can be comfortable in the knowledge that they won't be upset that you have decided to curl up on the sofa with a bottle of red while yelling at the football.
They won't be upset because they've forgotten who you are anyway.
You won't disappoint your children when they ask you for money, because even they realise that by 45 no one has a brass razoo . . . unless they're on TV.
I did a lot of research about turning 45 so I could alert readers about the benefits. I logged on to the Internet and typed the words ?45th birthday? into a search engine, guessing that with 800 million Web pages of information supplied by more than 100 million people, someone, somewhere would have something wise to say about being 45; something wise I could share with you.
What I found was the same joke repeated on more than 50 different pages. It is entitled ?Why I sacked my secretary on my 45th birthday?, and goes like this: ?On my 45th birthday I wasn't feeling too hot. At breakfast my wife didn't say anything about my birthday, let alone give me a present.
?I thought the children would certainly remember and smother me with birthday kisses and gifts. But not a word.
?However, when I walked into my office, my secretary said, "Good morning, boss, happy birthday', and I felt a bit better. About noon my secretary said: "It's such a beautiful day and it's your birthday, let's go to lunch'.
?So we went out into the country to a little private place. We had two martinis and enjoyed lunch tremendously. On the way back to the office, she said: "It's such a beautiful day. We don't need to go back to the office. Let's go by my place and I will fix you another martini'.
?So we went to her place, we enjoyed another martini and she said: "Boss, if you don't mind, I think I'll go into the bedroom and slip into something more comfortable'. I didn't mind at all.
?About six minutes later she came out of the bedroom . . . carrying a big birthday cake, followed by my wife and children. All were singing Happy Birthday. ?And there I sat . . . with nothing on except my socks.?
Forty-five. It's a tough age.
