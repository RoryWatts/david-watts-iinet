Aussie.com a hit with surfers 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 24-09-1996

 A PERTH-BASED search engine is attracting so many hits that Netscape wants to make it one of its three recommended Australian World Wide Web sites.
Aussie.com, whose search engine is catchily named aussie.com.au (just think of the cricket anthem Come on Aussie Come On) is now the most visited in Australia, according to cooperator Brian Fletcher.
He says Netscape has asked if aussie.com.au can handle a million hits a day, the figure the browser software giant predicts will be attracted by placement in the top three list.
Netscape placement is part of a strategy to lift the company's Australian profile when it sets up regional headquarters in Melbourne.
Mr Fletcher is confident Aussie.com can deliver. The company is now implementing a bigger, faster engine which he terms a ?super Aussie search?.
Aussie.com was set up in 1994 by self-described Net enthusiast Rudy Vogelsang, who called his Web index the AAA index.
?Pure altruism was his motive, plus, it was a vehicle for his brilliant Web page making skills,? Mr Fletcher said. ?It demanded more and more of his time just to keep it maintained.
?I was invited in as an investor. I provided funding for upgraded hardware and programming.?
The company is betting on the Web becoming a thriving commercial enterprise.
?We are concentrating on building the best index, with the highest traffic, which we have already achieved,? Mr Fletcher says.
The importance of aussie.com.au is that it searches only for Australian Web pages and offers Australian businesses reasonably cheap advertising on the Net.
