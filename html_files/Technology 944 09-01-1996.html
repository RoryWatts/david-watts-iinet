WA whiz kids are not young after all 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 09-01-1996

 A SURVEY of Internet use in WA demolishes the image of nerdy, pimple-prone geeks hunched over keyboards.
Access provider iiNet Technologies did an online survey late last year. Of 286 respondents, 27 per cent were aged 30-40 and 25 per cent 40-50.
The age group commonly thought to be the most computer literate - 18-25-year-olds - comprised only 18 per cent of those surveyed.
The testosterone factor was immense with a male-female ratio of 91 per cent to 9 per cent.
Nearly half of the users operated with Windows, 35 per cent with Windows 95, 7 per cent had Macs and 4 per cent used the once-favoured Unix.
More than half (51 per cent) rated the World Wide Web as their main Net use, 27 per cent said e-mail and 11 per cent said newsgroups. Only 3 per cent rated relay chat as their main use - the same number as File Transfer Protocol users.
Connection speed was rated the biggest problem by 44 per cent.
