No Web of intrigue for WA Parliament 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 05-03-1996

 THE House on the Hill is now the House on the information highway. The WA Parliament went live on the Internet last month with a commitment to spread democracy in cyberspace.
In a simple, but effective World Wide Web site, Parliament is presented with a picture of the House in all its night-time glory.
Lists of Members of the Legislative Assembly and Council are given and are available in Adobe PDF format for ?picture perfect electronic copies of documents?.
Phone and fax numbers are given for MPs, but there are no e-mail addresses. Hyperlinks are used to take browsers to Ministers' biographies and pictures, and there is a link to the Web home page of Greens MLC Jim Scott.
At least one MLA, Whitford Liberal Rob Johnson, has an Internet site of his own.
The Parliament site links to another new one for the State Government - the Commission on Government.
URL: http://www.wa.gov.au/parl/
