Bum deal in battle of sexes 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 20-06-1997

 THE gorgeous symbol of our State, Elle Macpherson, wants a family. Many blokes I know would be prepared to help her. However, I suspect their interest stops at a vital point.
They're quite happy to practise with Elle at making a family. When it comes to the big step into fatherhood, even with the body beautiful, it is probably too daunting.
Fatherhood is not easy. Dads have a lot to do:
lThey have to organise the kiddies to help mum with the washing-up.
lThey have to tell the lawnmower man where he can lower the blades and which weeds to leave.
lThey are responsible for pulling mice out of traps.
lThey have to ensure their stubbies are well organised in the fridge so some room is left for trifles like food.
lThey have to organise the rest of the family's viewing habits so they don't intrude on the footy, cricket and/or Wimbledon (it's an exhausting time of the year).
All this can seem such awesome responsibility. The fact that men do not, as a rule, seek the company of other men to unburden themselves over a cappuccino and focaccia, also means most are incapable of explaining just how hard fatherhood, and even manhood, is.
To complicate matters, women are demanding more of a say in the running of the world. Chaps often feel they get a pretty fair say as it is, but let's be reasonable, that is often only in matters concerning the running of the household.
Men run the greater world and this is where women are demanding the say.
MY FEELING is they can have an equal say when they can deliver a shirt front like John Worsfold. However, this has not gone over well with a number of GYMs (gorgeous young mums) who have been on the receiving end of my wisdom. In fact they looked like they wanted to deliver a Woosha shirt front there and then.
Recently I heard a new suggestion to promote equality of the sexes. It was both novel and revolutionary, and made by a man of good character who shall remain nameless.
He opined that true equality of the sexes could not be achieved until toilet seats in all mixed households were left at a 45-degree angle.
Men could not complain when answering a call of nature that the toilet seat was always down. Conversely, women could not yell that the toilet seat was always up when they went for a quiet sit.
One huge snag in this otherwise inspired suggestion was that the toilet seat was actually fixed in that position, ie, it was a permanent fixture.
This presents a few practical problems. Men would be better off because they can at least aim for the bowl, choosing to go either over or under the 45-degree seat. It would present a sharp-shooting challenge that most blokes could not refuse.
The problems would come at the end of the loo session when the forces of nature weakened to a quiet trickle . . . especially for those who had chosen to go over the top.
Women would have a bigger challenge. They would have to balance precariously at an angle, praying that all those diets were working.
Men would suffer the same fate once or twice a day but it would stop them reading the paper to get out of the domestic chores.
Somehow I think it's back to the drawing board.
NOW for a wee joke (no it's not the same subject as before). To ward off the lawyers, let me stress that this is a figment of imagination. It never happened . . . OK.
Our smiley Federal Treasurer Peter Costello was at a cocktail party for a group of stockbrokers. In typical politician fashion, he cornered a senior executive to brag about his economic programs.
Mr Costello: You know, Bruce, the economy is looking so good. If I weren't Treasurer I'd buy some shares.
Bruce: You know, Peter, I agree with you. If you weren't Treasurer I'd buy some, too.
