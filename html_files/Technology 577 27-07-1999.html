AdultShop unveils lust for world supremacy 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 27-07-1999

 PERTH online company AdultShop.com has a simple plan - it would like to dominate the world.
Its plan revolves around sex, easily the biggest money-spinner on the Internet.
Malcolm Day, the managing director of Western Minerals which will change its name to AdultShop.com in a few weeks, sees rich potential in selling adult goods such as videos, books and sex toys from its Web site.
AdultShop.com's site is not expected to be launched officially until December.
But in the month since it first appeared on the Web - with one steamy picture and an invitation to register interest - thousands have entered their e-mail addresses so they can keep abreast of AdultShop's growth.
Mr Day told The West Australian in an e-mail interview the aim was to dominate the online sex shop market.
The Eros Foundation has estimated that Australians spend $180 million a year on adult products, $50 million of that through mail order.
The global online adult entertainment industry is estimated at $10 billion a year. Mr Day says AdultShop only faces competition from a few small online shops.
?These are small non-professional sites which offer a limited product range,? he said. ?There are no Aussie sites with any market dominance. Similarly, there are no such sites with any world dominance. That's our optimistic plan - to have world dominance.?
The strategy is simple: target a huge but highly fragmented global market; establish a global brand through the Internet, and sit back while the money pours in.
AdultShop expects a lot of its trade will come from people who might be too embarrassed to walk into a sex shop in the so-called real world, and are more comfortable ordering over the Web.
Mr Day said AdultShop would try to form alliances with other adult products companies worldwide so overseas orders could be filled easily.
AdultShop will also have the resources of 21 Barbarellas stores behind it. Mr Day is a director of Barbarellas and AdultShop is basically the child of that well-known WA sex shop.
Mr Day, a 34-year-old civil engineer, drifted into the sex shop business after a three-month holiday in the US in 1994.
He returned to Australia with the idea of introducing such businesses as phone sex and dating services here. He built a computerised messaging service, where lonely people could leave a brief message about themselves along with a phone number.
He then approached Barbarellas and the two entered into a joint venture and began developing an e-commerce site - AdultShop.com.
AdultShop's stock has been trading on the stock exchange for a month. It found its way to the market through a takeover of gold miner Western Minerals, which suspended trading in late March at 5.8¢ a share. When WestMin - Mr Day's shelf company for AdultShop - resumed trading in June, the shares jumped to about 25¢. They are now in the high 30s.
One possible snag facing AdultShop is the Government's Net censorship laws which will come into force on January 1.
The laws will allow the Australian Broadcasting Authority to rate sites. An X-rating will mean the site cannot be accessed by Australians, and R-rated sites will be accessible only through age-verification programs.
Mr Day said he did not expect AdultShop to be rated X, but there were plans for an R-rated, adults-only section of the site that would sell magazines and videos.
