Firms sight better way to do business 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 25-06-1996

 WA COMPANIES are using the Internet as a business tool in widely different ways.
Most computer firms, whether they specialise in software, hardware or both, have had a presence on the World Wide Web for some time. Downloading software is still a major business transaction on the Web.
But recently firms with interests as varied as selling cars, weddings, wildflowers and stuffed bears have been learning HTML and going where they once feared to tread.
Prosser Toyota, the State's oldest Toyota dealership, has gone beyond the normal Web presence of a site and offers a catalogue that can be downloaded to live on a user's hard drive.
?The forte of the Internet is information delivery, and in keeping with the expectations of a diverse viewer population we have delivered a very graphic, information-rich site,? Prosser Toyota director Clive Prosser says.
?This might, at first, seem like standard Internet fare, however, what makes our site unique is a range of downloadable electronic products.?
Prosser offers Windows wallpaper background showing the Toyota range, screensavers and an electronic catalogue of new and used cars.
The catalogue contains more than 56MB of compressed information that uses less than 3MB when downloaded and executed on a hard drive.
Mr Prosser says the Web site and electronic catalogue have attracted inquiries from the US, Britain, and even Japan, the home of Toyota.

JAPAN is also in the sites of a vastly different WA company.
Australian Dream Weddings of Nedlands virtually forgets its Australian origins at its Web site.
Each of Australian Dream Weddings' pages is almost entirely in Japanese, designed to attract Japanese here to be wed in glamorous style.
Each page contains a GIF image of a moment from a ?dream? wedding - Rolls-Royce pulling up outside a luxury hotel to collect the bride, for example - with accompanying text in Japanese.
It is a simple, clever idea which amounts to nothing more than billboards in cyberspace, but neatly done to attract the prospective overseas clientele.
