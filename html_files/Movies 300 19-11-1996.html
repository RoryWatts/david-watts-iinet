More than you need to know about The Phantom 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 19-11-1996

 PHANTOM is good, clean fun. It could just make an Old Jungle Saying and be applied to the film that has been popular in Perth for the past few months.
The film is not bad at bringing the comic-book hero to technicolor life, but for the real low-down on the Ghost Who Walks you need to visit a couple of sites on the Web.
The Phantom site, officially tied up with the Paramount film, presents a tour chock full of trivia. Surfers can enter the Skull Cave and explore each main treasure therein.
For example, a simple click on the Phantom's throne will bring up a story about the throne's origins and those of the Chain of Patience draped on it.
More tall tales emanate from the Treasure Room where the Phantom not only has King Arthur's sword, Excalibur, but also a cup made out of one gigantic diamond.
The best place to visit is the Chronicles, where by clicking on one of 21 volumes you can read the biography of most of the Phantoms.
Each story is as unbelievable as the next, particularly the one where the 19th Phantom becalmed a bull elephant, moved a boulder the size of a house, then successfully wrestled a 3m giant - all in three days - but they are fun.
Another Phantom site - The Ghost Who Walks - maintained in Australia is a different sort of treasure trove listing every Phantom comic-book storyline in the past 60 years, relating fascinating trivia about the Phantom suit's real colour (it should have been grey), and summarising the life of Phantom creator Lee Falk.
The big disappointment at both sites seems to be a lack of the full list of Old Jungle Sayings; indispensable in any serious look at the superhero.
