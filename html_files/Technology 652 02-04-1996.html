Viruses spawn a geek at light side 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 02-04-1996

 GEEK humour isn't too bad when you get used to it - you just have to be totally absorbed in computers.
The US magazine, Information Week, put out a list of new viruses recently. They include:
? Paul Revere Virus: This revolutionary virus doesn't horse around. It warns you of impending hard disk attack: One if by LAN, two if by C:/.
? Congressional Virus: The computer locks up, and the screen splits erratically with a message appearing on each half of the monitor blaming the other side for the problem.
? Freudian Virus: Your computer becomes obsessed with its own motherboard.
The US Internet giant c/net publishes a regular bulletin which includes bizarre top 10 lists.
Last week it produced the top 10 signs of talking to a PC slacker.
c/net defined slackers as people who spend hours working very hard at utterly trivial tasks.
Some sayings which give the game away:
? ?Playing Solitaire? No. I'm honing my drag-and-drop skills while developing my deductive reasoning.?
? ?My productivity will increase as soon as I find the right colour scheme and wallpaper for my desktop.?
? ?Anyone can reboot a PC. I'm working on a visual basic script that can do it from a taskbar button in Excel.?
