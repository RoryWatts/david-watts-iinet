League rates well with hot-blooded males 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 31-03-1998

 RUGBY league is back on our screens, and boy is it hot.
The reunited game, fresh from the mad disruption of Rupert Murdoch's attempt to control sport in every continent, had been unsighted until 11.45pm on Friday.
Even though Channel 9 here waited until round three to show the newly named National Rugby League competition, it returned with a bang.
I think Balmain upset Manly 18-4 in front of a passionate home crowd.
But details became elusive because of the nature of the ads that littered the game.
One was for a phone-sex outfit, though why anyone would want to have sex with a phone is hard to imagine. Another was from the Hustler organisation and featured a room crammed with scantily clad young women.
There were about a dozen of them but they had barely enough clothes for one.
The final shot in this ad featured a blonde so scantily clad that she had nothing on at all ... except the routine leer as she picked up the phone.
It certainly made a change from listening to Ewen McCormick's theories about real football.
Parents who intend taping league for their children might want to do some early-morning editing if this trend continues.
Of course, the rugby league was a mere sideshow to the real sporting action - round one of the AFL season on Channel 7.
It was strangely flat. The North-West Coast opener was a beauty - what is the betting that Adam Simpson could ever repeat his match-winning, left-footed snap - but the rest of the round was mostly indifferent.
Carlton-Adelaide was a dull game with an exciting finish. Boundary rider Neil Kerley had to eat his half-time words of ?people who say Adelaide is a soft side are wrong?.
St Kilda-Geelong was typical Waverley stuff. The Cats got lost in the open spaces.
We saw four or five shots of Collingwood-Hawthorn on the AFL Winners, but got more of Carlton-Adelaide and North-West Coast, games which had already been given full cover.
And the constant crosses to Yvette Mooney for news updates was irritating on a slow news day when she had little to say.
BULLDOGS-Brisbane was surprisingly one-sided. But Port-Sydney on Sunday was pretty good.
Fremantle-Melbourne was really one for Dockers fans. The heat sucked the skills away.
And we hardly saw Richmond-Essendon, the game that attracted the punters (70,000 of them) and stunned the critics, who have been rating the Bombers as premiership contenders.
What we did get was a lot of football. And Seven has made some improvements, particularly with its goal-line camera angles.
There also appears to be a trend towards more analysis, giving Tim Watson, Leigh Matthews and Gerard Healy more of a say. Doug Hawkins made his expert commentator debut and seems as likeable a character as he was when court jester on the Footy Show.
Fourth umpire John Russo is being used well, but is it any real surprise that even he seems bewildered by about one-third of the decisions?
And there wasn't a scantily clad woman with a phone to be seen.
