Site makes those who have, reflect on those who don't

Date: 11-08-1998

SOCIETY
Brotherhood of St Laurence poverty report
home.vicnet.net.au/~bstl/
povupdte.htm
A MONG the millions of pages of trivia and self-indulgence on the web, there are sites which can pull us up and make us think. The Brotherhood of St Laurence's poverty page will not win awards for design - it is far too textual - but it might help make Netizens sit back and think how fortunate they are.
The page says 11 per cent of the world's population lives in poverty. It also has a detailed guide to what constitutes the poverty line.
SPORT
www.formula1.com (motor racing)
T HIS unofficial site covers everything that moves on the grand prix circuit and is brimming with the latest news and pictures.
Formula One nuts can call up maps of all the circuits and read the excellent profiles of the drivers, their cars and their teams.
They can also enter a fantasy competition to manage a racing team, or vote for the most gorgeous woman seen trackside.
COMMUNICATIONS
Phone directories on the web
www.contractjobs.com/tel/
T ELEPHONE directories on the web calls itself the Internet's original and most detailed index of online phone books. It links to Yellow Pages, white pages, business directories, e-mail addresses and fax listings around the world.
Directories are found easily under the regional categories: the Americas, Europe and Africa, Asia-Pacific, and - for e-mail - worldwide and cyberspace.
STRANGE
News of the Weird
www.newsoftheweird.com
T HIS is the online compilation of journalist Chuck Shepherd's column, hyped as ?the number one source for bizarre-but-true news?.
Shepherd syndicates the column to 300 publications and is celebrating its 10th anniversary this year.
To mark that anniversary, Shepherd's boss, Universal Press Syndicate, has decided to unleash his strange-but-true tales on the web.
Surfers can search for topics that they suspect generate weird stories, or find them listed conveniently under headings such as animals or Washington.
