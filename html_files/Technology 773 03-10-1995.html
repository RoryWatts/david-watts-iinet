Webcrawler tracks Carmen down 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 03-10-1995

 I REALLY can't recall, but I believe Carmen Lawrence is a big name. But how big?
The World Wide Web surely should be able to tell us.
Using the search tools, Lycos, Yahoo Search and Webcrawler, the words Carmen Lawrence unearthed some interesting, even baffling, results.
She simply doesn't exist, according to Yahoo. But Lycos found 6238 documents with either Carmen or Lawrence mentioned.
It summarised the first 10 of these 6238 findings, and lo and behold the first two of the 10 results were from the same document: Introduction to this special issue on computer-supported cooperative work.
That's one for the bookmarks.
Something told me this wasn't our Carmen, Member for Fremantle and Federal Health Minister.
Life was too short to check the other 6236 results, so bring in the trusty Webcrawler.
Sanity, at last.
Nineteen documents, with the third one, Cabinet and the Ministry. This was indeed her, along with Paul Keating, Kim Beazley, Gareth Evans et al, and accompanying photographs and potted biographies.
Cause for more than a little bewilderment was that this search result was only number three.
The two entries above it were Midwifery, Pregnancy and Birth Related Information and Investigadores del Centro.
Curiouser and curiouser on the list of 19 was number 9, The Miss Piggy Page, number 18, the Vampyres Film List and lucky last number 19, http://gip.eecs.umich. edu/OO/FAQ/Recreation/Star-Trek
.Actors-Roles.Part3.1.
So Dr Lawrence is really a Klingon ... enough of that. Beam me up Scottie, there's precious little intelligent life down here, and in the interests of bipartisanship I sought Webcrawler's response to the request Richard Court.
Naturally the word Court caused paroxysms of search mania, with the result being 349 finds.
Nestled among the first 25 results were the fascinatingly boring titles: About the court and judges and Bankruptcy creditors' service Inc.
More intriguing, but way off the path to the Premier's door was The Rec. Nude Legal FAQ, rev. 7/1/95.
Ah ha! Richard the naked truth, but no. Further up the list was Richard's Home Page.
Paydirt, I thought. Mr Court has joined the illustrious company of his sworn enemy Prime Minister Paul Keating, who has his own home page, but which has not been updated since June.
Still it's nice to have a photograph of the PM on his every utterance.
But Richard's Home Page was no such effort by the Premier. No, it was Curtin student Richard Giles extolling the virtues of his faculty and the Bentley Technology Park.
