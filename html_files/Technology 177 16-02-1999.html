Ads emerge from shadow of eclipse 

Author: NICK MILLER; DAVID WATTS

Date: 16-02-1999

 A PERTH Internet advertising business is using today's eclipse to boost traffic to its site.
Internet service provider Ace Online is experimenting with using current affairs to attract people to its PerthWA Shopping Guide, a site for Perth and WA businesses to advertise their products and services.
As well as running a permanent live camera that captures pictures of Perth 24 hours a day, Ace will run a special five-camera ?Webcast? of today's eclipse, at sun.livecam.com.au.
Ace manager Nigel Sutherland said projects like these were important to attract people's attention to the shopping guide.
?We started doing this sort of project to attract traffic through the site,? he said.
?It's easy to get lost on the Internet, and we are trying to get local people to use (our site) - we're not extremely philanthropic, we have to attract advertisers.
?We had 18,000 hits per minute during the Skyworks, and the eclipse should be big too.?
Two of the video cameras will have strong filters letting them look directly at the sun.
The filters are strong enough so that the sun appears as a pale yellow disc, with features such as sunspots clearly visible. Three other cameras will cover the rest of Perth, to show the dimming of the natural light.
The show will run from 2pm until 5pm today.
The eclipse Web page has plenty of links through to the shopping guide, with the aim of presenting the site as a ?portal? into WA.
Ace is undertaking another big project at the moment - building a virtual copy of Perth's shopping centres.
Ace has gone down every street of the CBD, taking pictures of the shopfronts and adding basic contact details to the picture on the Internet - free.
If shops wish to, they can pay for a more sophisticated link to their own Web site.
Mr Sutherland said this was another way of attracting traffic as well as advertisers. He aimed to cover all the major retail and business concentrations in Perth.
