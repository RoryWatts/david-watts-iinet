Website first for public transport 

Author: TORRANCE MENDEZ; DAVID WATTS

Date: 09-02-1998

 PERTH people can now surf the Net to find the best way to get anywhere in the city on public transport.
Dr Chris Whitaker, director-general of the Department of Transport, said the timetable initiative was just the beginning of much more computerised information.
The department has made available an interactive bus, ferry and train journey planner and says commuters, tourists and day-trippers can now key in the addresses of departure and arrival points and be shown five different ways to make the journey on public transport.
They can find out how long the journey will take and even how far they must walk to a bus stop or other connection.
The move makes Perth the only Australian city with an interactive journey planner on the World Wide Web of the Internet.
Before the launch of the website, people wanting timetable information mostly relied on printed versions or the Serco telephone information facility.
The service employs a battery of 30 phone operators distributing information to callers from a computerised database in the busport in Mounts Bay Road.
Transperth spent $60,000 modifying the database and placed it on the Internet for anyone to view for the price of a local phone call.
?We're also developing the ability for Internet users to pay for their vehicle registration on-line and even design their own number plates,? Dr Whitaker said.
?We're looking at developing a site which will provide information like tidal movements and where surfers can find the best waves,? he said.
Transperth plans to set up screens in big shops and other busy locations as part of a drive to attract more people to public transport.
Sydney does not have an interactive journey planner like Perth's though it does offer electronic versions of train and ferry timetables.
Planners similar to Transperth's have been used in Britain for the past 13 months by both British Rail and the London Underground.
But neither produces results that bring together all forms of public transport.
The journey planner is on: www.transperth.wa.gov.au
