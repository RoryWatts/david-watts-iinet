It's all there on the Net 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 01-09-1998

 DISAPPOINTED West Coast supporters can shake off reality for a few days with a nifty Internet calculator available on the World Wide Web.
There they will find the AFL Final 8 Calculator site which shows it is possible to conjure up a West Coast v Adelaide grand final on September 26.
This nightmare scenario for Victorians is highly unlikely in real life, but theoretically possible. The starting point is for both to win this weekend, of course.
The calculator takes the pain out of trying to work out the McIntyre final eight permutations with a fixture card and a pencil.
It is a must-have tool for the diehard Internet football fan once the finals get under way, and fun for the dreamers until then. Another must-have is the All-Time AFL Ladder, which covers all teams from 1897 to the latest round, and shows their position.
The top four since 1897 are: Collingwood with a win/loss ratio of 62.46, followed by Carlton (62.16), West Coast (61.62) and Essendon (58.12).
The wooden spooner is the long-defunct University team with a ratio of 22.22. Brisbane is bottom of present clubs with 33.08.
Breakdowns of the ladder into 10-year segments are also available.
