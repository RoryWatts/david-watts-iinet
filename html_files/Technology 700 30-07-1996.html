Sex is off the menu: survey 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 30-07-1996

 SEX is on the way out, or maybe it just isn't as big as it's cracked up to be.
A recent US Internet survey by the NPD PC-Meter shows that adult sites on the World Wide Web are being visited far less frequently.
The NPD PC-Meter is a piece of equipment attached to the computers in 4000 US homes, similar to a TV ratings meter. The latest published survey was for the second quarter of the year.
It shows that of the top-50 content-provided sites on the Web - as opposed to search engines and directories - only one, playboy.com, is an adult site.
Adult content was visited by 19.4 per cent of the surveyed audience, compared with 25.5 per cent in the previous survey. Woman surfers - up from 31.7 per cent to 38.7 - is believed responsible for the drop.
Education sites remain the most visited on the Web (45 per cent). Information, news and entertainment sites, led by Pathfinder and c/net, come second with 41.5 per cent of hits, then adult sites followed by government.
