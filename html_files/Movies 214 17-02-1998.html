 KIDS LITERATURE
Winnie the Pooh and Tigger Too!
www.oh-no.com/pooh.html
A.A. Milne may be turning in his grave at what Disney has done to his creative masterpiece Winnie the Pooh. But there are few complaints among the target audience - children (and often their parents). Pooh, the bear of very little brain, his minder Christopher Robin, Tigger, Piglet, Owl and the rest, sprang to literary life in 1926 as the product of bedtime stories that Milne told his son Christopher. Disney bought the rights to the Pooh stories and characters and animated their adventures - occasionally remaining faithful to the Milne originalsThis site devotes much of its copious space to the animated versions, but has much on the origins of Pooh too. Tigger the toy tiger gets almost equal billing because the site creator's wife is said to be bananas about him. (DW)

COMMUNITY
Mt Lawley Neighbourhood Learning Centre
www.icenet.com.au/ ~mtlawlc/
If you live in the Mt Lawley area and are thinking of taking up a new course or hobby, look no further than your neighbourhood learning centre. The volunteer-run and non-profit centre based in a 1920s residence on Beaufort Street celebrates its 20th year of operation this year and is said to be the first Web site for a community adult learning centre in WA.The Web site was created by multimedia students from Edith Cowan University and is now being maintained by the centre. Courses on offer include aromatherapy, breadmaking, parent effectiveness training and social tennis. Each course has a brief description, times and costs.A news section has dates to remember such as school terms, the centre's AGM and course enrolment dates.(SY)
COMMUNITY
Perth City Mission Summer Sleepout
www.pcm.org.au
Give up your bed for the night, round up some sponsors and help raise money for the Perth City Mission's youth programs by participating in the Sizzling Summer Sleepout.
The sleepout takes place from 8pm on Sunday March 1 at at Hillarys Boat Harbour. For your minimum sponsorship of $25 (payable prior to admission), you get free admission, a snack and drink and use of facilities such as waterslides, garden golf and trampolines.Other information at the site includes history, key contacts and details of other Perth City Mission projects. (SY)
