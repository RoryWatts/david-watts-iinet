Net takes to Telstra sell-off 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 26-02-1996

 OPPOSITION Leader John Howard's planned part sale of Telstra and the prospect of Alexander Downer as foreign affairs minister are the biggest talking points on Internet newsgroups.
The most talkative newsgroup in the campaign is aus.politics, which receives more than 100 postings a day on a wide variety of topics.
The newsgroup had been dominated by perhaps 10 men who appeared to favour conservative thinking. But in the past week, there has been more input from Labor supporters.
The discussion on Mr Howard's plan to spend $1 billion on the environment by selling a third of Telstra is the biggest issue.
The initial thread was that Mr Howard would have to blackmail Greens (WA) and Democrat senators to get Telstra sell-off legislation through.
One correspondent wrote: ?I am worried about the whole picture coming together. I see Howard promising to run a deficit to make good on middle-class welfare but no promise to make good on the environment if the Senate crossbenches don't support Telstra privatisation.?
Response: ?Why's that? Because you believe more in Telstra than the environment, obviously.?
The Downer as foreign affairs minister topic began with a Liberal supporter expressing concern.
Others agreed, fearing a repetition of Mr Downer's ?things that batter? statement at an international forum.
These fears gave way to speculation that Mr Downer was shadow foreign affairs minister only as a consolation for losing the party leadership and the portfolio would go to someone like Senate Opposition Leader Robert Hill if the coalition won.
After about 50 postings, the subject changed to a tipping competition on the election result - about 50-50 Labor-Liberal.
