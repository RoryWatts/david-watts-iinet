Time for a few home truths 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 04-08-1998

 ONE of the few dead certainties in football is that the game is now being bred for the couch potato.
The development of Subiaco Oval as a 43,500 all-seat stadium, announced last week, simply confirms that.
It means in two or three years most home-and-away games will be played at sold-out, so-called boutique venues - Subiaco, Football Park, the new Docklands and the SCG most prominently - and telecast live.
It is a good time to focus on what Channel 7 can do to improve its heavy and mostly good coverage.
As the great C.P. Scott said when editor of the old Manchester Guardian: ?Opinions are free, but facts are sacred.?
These are opinions. Feel free to send your own to the addresses at the end of the column.
1: CONTINUE to try to unearth new commentators. Another weekend, the same old voices ... it's not quite true, but sometimes it feels like it.
Experiments this year with Paul Couch, Neil Brooks and Graham Cornes need more work.
2: CUT the size of the commentary box. We do not need a host, a main caller, a back-up, an analyst, a boundary rider and a fourth umpire.
A main caller, an offsider-analyst and a boundary man would cut the incessant chatter and direct more attention to the vital ingredient of football ... the game.
3: GET rid of the fourth umpire. Who watches football to hear about the umpires? John Russo's nattering about being baffled by both decisions and non-decisions took a lot of pleasure out of watching Essendon steal the game from Carlton on Sunday.
4: MAKE the boundary riders neutral. It is time Neil Kerley was sent to a non-Adelaide match. Ditto for Adrian Barich and the Eagles or Dockers.
Little seems to be gained by blatant one-sided opinion. There is plenty of that in the living room already. Leave something for the fans, please.
5: GO easy on the ads. Do we really need advertisements after every other goal and in batches of five or six at the breaks?
Virtual advertising may be the way to go if the details can be tied up with the likes of the Melbourne Cricket Club.
A good half-time show like the ABC's Half-Time at the Footy during Westar Rules coverage would be preferable to what Seven offers.
6: BE judicious with replays. Slow-motion replays can enhance the drama and help explain what really happened in the heat of the moment.
But when they interrupt live coverage, they are a pain.
7: SEVEN should not try to compete with Sam Newman. There is only one Footy Show, and even if it was below par last week - perhaps there is a home ground advantage - Live and Kicking cannot measure up to the ad-libs, profanity and occasional genuine hard footy news that Nine's offering delivers.
