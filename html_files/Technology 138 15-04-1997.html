Few bugs in early release 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 15-04-1997

 MICROSOFT'S Internet Explorer 4.0 is meant for developers and experienced users only at this stage. Microsoft makes that clear at its download site.
But judging by how hard it is to get to that site and the growing number of comments in newsgroups, many people are trying out the exciting new program.
Microsoft makes its warning because MSIE 4.0 is not even in beta release and could be prone to bugs, lock-ups and crashes. That has certainly been the case with Netscape Communicator's pre-release. A third, less buggy version appeared last week.
But WestWeb has had a smooth ride with the preview release. There have been two or three lock-ups on the Web, but no general protection faults or total freezes yet.
The reaction from HotWired's columnist was: ?Internet Explorer 4.0 is good software. Really good. Way better than Netscape. I suspect a lot of reviewers will be apologising for their good reviews.?
