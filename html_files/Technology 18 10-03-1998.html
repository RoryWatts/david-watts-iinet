Author: DAVID WATTS; SUE YEAP

Date: 10-03-1998

 www.oscar.com
So what's it to be this year? Titanic, As Good as it Gets, The Full Monty ? Find out all the latest glitter and gossip at this official Oscar site. It has a thorough history of the back-slapping Hollywood tradition, lists of all this year's nominees, latest news on Billy Crystal's gag writers and online games that enable browsers to join a tipping contest or try their talents at fashion trivia. A must for movie buffs. (DW)
ColorCenter
www.hidaho.com/colorcenter/
intro.html
The ColorCenter uses JavaScript more intelligently than nearly any other site on the Web. Its aim is to show HTML authors what different colour combinations - of text, background and links - will look like online.It displays an easy-to-use colour palette which can be clicked on after selecting which property you would like to colour. Results are instantaneous and include the exact HTML coding that produces the mixture. This coding can be copied then pasted into a HTML document.The site also enables you to visit other links to pick up different images to test them against colour schemes. Version 3 or better of both Netscape or Explorer is recommended for best results. (DW)
Information Please
www.infoplease.com
Here is a search engine that looks up encyclopaedias, dictionaries and almanacs to deliver answers. The reference material is authoritative and, more importantly, easily accessible because it is online.Information Please's pedigree goes back to 1938. Between then and 1952, a radio quiz show of the same name hosted guests of the stature of Alfred Hitchcock, Will Rogers, Leonard Bernstein, Gracie Allen and Orson Welles.In 1947, the show released its first Information Please Almanac. It has been published annually since. Described as the ?ultimate fact finder on the Web?, the site also draws from almanacs on sports, entertainment, business, and kids.
Just like the big search engines - Yahoo, AltaVista, etc - Information Please is fast and easy to use. It promises answers to questions on anything from Apples to Zimbabwe. (DW)
Asian Movie Festival
www.reel.com/festivals/
festival.html
You've just seen The Replacement Killers and now want to see other movies starring Chow Yun-Fat. Maybe you've seen Tarantino's latest, Jackie Brown, and didn't understood Samuel L Jackson's references to the guns used in The Killer.If you can't get hold of Asian movies at your local video store, then step right up to Reel.Com's online Asian movie festival, packed with information about the films of Chow, recent Bond girl Michelle Yeoh, Jackie Chan, director John Woo and more.Many of the movies are available for sale and feature a brief plot plus a guide to the amounts of sex, violence, drama, action, romance and so on. Movies are helpfully cross-matched so you can find films with similar themes, stars, etc. (SY)
duJour.Com
www.dujour.com
If you have been looking for ?Internet nirvana?, then step right up to duJour, a site nominated by reader Joel Fraser.Aimed at trivia and games fanatics, each day duJour - as its name implies - serves up Riddle du Jour, MondoTrivia and assorted brain teasers. Answer them correctly and you could win a prize provided one of the site's sponsors ( a ?subtle? form of advertising).Some contests attract thousands of entrants. To enter any of them you need a special registration code, which is free.
One of the games that has seen many users defeated is The Labyrinth, where you play Theseus and save Athens from the ravages of King Minos and his pet Minotaur. The task at hand is to is to navigate through The Labyrinth's ?rooms?, find the monster's lair and, you guessed it, win a prize from one of duJour's sponsors.
A fun section with no prizes or sponsors attached - yet - is GIFspiel. It features two photos that have been morphed and you have to guess who the originals were.
Even if you don't feel up to pitting your brains against the wits at duJour, Ce Jour offer information about births, deaths, special events and holidays for each day of the year. (SY)
