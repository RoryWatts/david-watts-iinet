Umpire Carey does it again 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 03-08-1999

 FREMANTLE Dockers supporter Shane Richmond was at a loose end the day after his side beat St Kilda in a Saturday night match at Subiaco last month.
Then, inspired by the highlight of that game, he went to his computer, virtually unpacked his Shockwave expertise, and designed a game that can be played on the Web.
The highlight of Fremantle-St Kilda was umpire Peter Carey's skilful chest mark during play which earned him a little ticking off from the AFL, as well as an almost permanent place on the TV news.
Mr Richmond, 23, came up with the Peter Carey game to add to an already humorous collection on display at his personal Web site.
Using Photoshop and Paintshop for the graphics, and Macromedia Director for the action, he put together a game which allows users to control umpire Carey with their mouse.
Their task is to manoeuvre Carey into a position where he can mark the pass from Docker Adrian Fletcher to his teammate Brad Wira.
If you cannot make the umpire get in the way after five attempts, you have to start all over again.
Mr Richmond runs a small Web development business called Blackboy Interactive, but says the creation of games such as the Peter Carey one has nothing to do with work.
He says he does not use Shockwave for clients usually. But he does use it - and expertise gained in receiving a post graduate certificate in interactive multimedia from Edith Cowan University - to amuse himself and brighten up his Dockers site.
Other games created by Mr Richmond include Virtual Willsy and Whack an Eagle. Web users have to help Andrew Wills kick a goal after the siren in a mythical grand final in Virtual Willsy. And in Whack an Eagle, they have to keep West Coast spies away from a Fremantle training session.
