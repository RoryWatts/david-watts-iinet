Bloke sheilas bloke blokes and two jokes 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 08-08-1997

 FOR the third week in a row I will talk about blokes. I wasn't going to, honestly. I actually wanted to write about something completely different . . . like TV, footy, beer or women.
But during the week I received a birthday card with a photo of a girl so pleasant to look at, it made me palpitate, pulsate and perspire.
She wore tiny white underwear and a scowl. Every part of her was both firm and smooth. Blokes love that. So of course I began to think of blokey things like the ultimate collection of women who appeal to blokes.
And lists are so addictive. I'll give up next week, doctor.
Bloke sheilas:
It sounds oxymoronic, like army intelligence, but this is just a list of women who make the average bloke palpitate, pulsate and perspire.
Sophia Loren: Those curves and those eyes are enough, but the lips and the accent make her irresistible.
Michelle Pfeiffer (as Catwoman in the film): The body-hugging leather makes blokes want to hug the body.
Julie Newmar (as Catwoman on TV): Ditto, but even more appealing because of her height and accent.
Geena Davis: Out-bloked even Bond and Arnie in The Long Kiss Goodnight in which she played an assassin with amnesia. She was unforgettable.
Helen Mirren: The thinking bloke's ideal companion. Won me when she told the Sunday Times in London how she started her day. ?If my chap's there we make love, then I listen to the BBC world news, then we make love again.?
Liz Hurley: Drop-dead gorgeous and sinfully curvaceous. Won blokes' sympathy when let down so publicly by her not very blokey boyfriend, Hugh Grant.
But enough of the girls. Blokes have role models, mostly from the world of sport. Let's take a look at them.
Bloke blokes:
Tony Lockett: No GYM (gorgeous young mum) wants her son to grow up to be like the 110kg Sydney Swans full-forward. But lots of blokes are in awe of his brute power and superb kicking. He also has a gentle wit, according to opposing full-backs. Witness these charming one-liners: ?How would you like to die in front of 35,000 people?? "If you touch my jumper, I'll kill ya.?
Glen Jakovich: The West Coast champion has a body that all blokes believe they see in the mirror every morning.
Steve Waugh: Tough, quiet and harsh on the Poms; an ideal Aussie bloke.
Dennis Lillee: Tough, exuberant and harsh on the Poms.
Roy Slavin: The more urbane and articulate member of Roy and H.G.
Dermott Brereton: A twinkle in the eye and a ripple in the bicep; what many aspire to.
Chris Mainwaring: Ditto with a WA angle.
Bloke etiquette. Traditional greeting to fellow bloke: Mate.
Happy agreement with fellow bloke (as in a response to ?Chris Lewis, hey?): Maaate.
Sad response or disagreement (response to something like ?Mate you ran off with my missus and you took my ute. Is the ute okay??): Maaaate.
At a public urinal: Look straight ahead. If caught glancing sideways, discuss the unfairness of Tony Lockett's latest suspension.
Washing hands after being at public urinal: Do not look longingly at reflection and comb hair. The next step is unthinkable. Soon you'll be using a blow-drier and opening a flower shop.
JUST enough space left for a few jokes. I've made them Irish jokes, but in one of them you could easily substitute Queenslander or New Zealander.
An American, an Australian and an Irishman were on Sale of the Century. The question: Complete the following and spell it. Old MacDonald had a ...
Buzzzz went the American. Old MacDonald had a ranch ... r-a-n-c-h.
Wrong, over to the Aussie. Old MacDonald had a station ... s-t-a-t-i-
o-n.
Wrong, what do you think Pat? Old MacDonald had a farm ... e-i-e-i-o.
And to finish:
Q: Who is Irish, white and lies around the pool all day?
A: Pati O'Furniture.
