Cricket: a reason for secession 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 24-03-1998

 THE Internet throws up some unsavoury content at times. Last Wednesday, a World Wide Web site called CricInfo was showing Australia 7-175 at tea in Calcutta.
Shocking. And it became more obscene as the days passed - Australia all out 233, India 3-369 (that was Thursday) and India 5-633 (on Friday).
Obscene is not a bad word to describe it. Try these as well: miserable, pathetic, puny, dumb, terrible, awful, shocking, horrible and bad.
Another word that hopefully is not too optimistic - aberration.
The Australians can hardly get any worse in the final Test which starts tomorrow in Bangalore.
A nation has been searching for answers. Captain Mark Taylor has been quoted as uttering a comment as profound as the one used by the truck driver in the Swan Brewery ad (?you've got a job, so you do it.?)
The skipper's answer on Saturday night was: ?We didn't make enough runs, or take enough wickets.?
Advice and explanations abound. At a Melbourne Age Internet site, fans have weighed in with their recipes for an Australian comeback in Bangalore.
Responses yesterday were dominated by Indians - why not; they dominated the Test?
Most said Shane Warne should swear off the beans. One helpful soul said Australia could win if it recalled Don Bradman.
The best thing about the debacle was that not much was seen on TV. Channel 9, normally berated for failing to cover anything outside Australia and England, has aired a couple of hours of lowlights.
Nine showed a huge, colourful, enthusiastic and knowledgeable crowd going bonkers over beautiful batting (Mohammad Azharuddin must qualify as one of the great artists).
On Sunday afternoon, Nine displayed a nice sense of irony by playing an hour of Australia's dreadful last innings, followed by live coverage of the Sheffield Shield final from the WACA Ground.
One minute viewers could cry in our beers as Greg Blewett, Michael Slater, Mark Waugh etc. surrendered meekly; the next they could raise the same glasses to the Warriors as they walloped Tasmania.
Cruelly, just as we were licking our lips in anticipation of a Brendon Julian onslaught - he had just made 119 between lunch and tea - he was out lbw second ball into the coverage.
The juxtaposition of the awful Aussies and the wonderful Warriors almost seemed a good argument for secession.
Opposition Leader Kim Beazley, a loyal WA cricket supporter, did not go quite that far.
But he did tell the ABC yesterday: ?I think that it is a terrific thing that the regular unfairness with which West Australian players are treated in national selections, means that WA has the opportunity to deliver punishment for it.?
