Shine climbs ladder of Net greats 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 04-03-1997

 AUSTRALIA'S Academy Award hope Shine has won global approval well ahead of the Oscar ceremonies on March 24.
The dramatised biography of eccentric Perth piano genius David Helfgott is rated the eighth most popular film at the Internet Movie Database, a repository of facts and trivia covering more than 50,000 films.
Internet users can cast votes for movies at the data base's site on the World Wide Web. They rate them on a scale of 1 (lousy) to 10 (excellent).
In the latest publication of the top 250, Net surfers have rated Shine better than critically acclaimed classics such as Casablanca, Citizen Kane, The Godfather and Pulp Fiction.
A total of 652 people had cast a vote for Shine at the time of the top 250 publication and given it an average score of 9.3.
The curators of the data base - all film buffs who give free time to maintain the electronic treasure trove - have weighted scores for all movies so that they have a common base. This helps clear up the distortions caused when 2000 people give one film an average of 7.5, and 250 people give another film an average of 8.5.
Mathematical weighting brings clearer averaging and levels the playing field.
On that basis, Shine's 9.3 has been converted to 8.7, putting it behind fellow Oscar nominee The English Patient, which has a weighted score of 8.8, gleaned from the 1217 voters whose average mark for the adaptation of the best-selling novel was 9.1.
Other nominees Jerry Maguire (21st) and Fargo (28th) also feature prominently on the list.
Shine's weighted vote puts it ahead of previous Oscar winners Schindler's List (14th), Braveheart (18th) and One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (23rd), among many others.
The top 250 list owes a lot to the demographics of Net users. Recent US surveys say that the average age of the world's 45 million Net population is 33 and the male-female ratio is 70-30.
The Net population has aged rapidly from an average 25 years old in 1994, and the sex quotient has changed dramatically since then too when the male-female ratio was 90-10.
But many movie votes were cast when the Net was younger and more testosterone-charged.
Thus the top 10 movies are: 1 Star Wars, 2 The Shawshank Redemption, 3 Trainspotting, 4 The English Patient, 5 The Wrong Trousers, 6 A Close Shave, 7 The Usual Suspects, 8 Shine, 9 Star Trek (First Contact) and 10 Casablanca.
Internet Movie Database can be found at: http://www.imdb.com
