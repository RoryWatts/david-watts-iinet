New version is slow monster to download 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 10-11-1998

 MICROSOFT'S new browser might be good, but it is a pain in the mouse to download and install.
The compressed file is 18,900k in size. It took me two hours 20 minutes to download it with a 28.8k modem. It then took another 10 minutes for the system to install it, restart and reconfigure itself.
It all looks deceptively simple. Go to the Microsoft site, say you want to download MSIE 5.0 beta, and the Windows automatic update page is summoned.
Nice if it worked, but it didn't on Thursday, the day of the release.
After five attempts and 30 minutes I went elsewhere (see Help Desk for alternative sites).
Users must first download a set-up file. At 452k, it takes only three minutes to run in your browser. It then prompts you to choose a download site for the main event - the 18,900k monster. The choice is wide and includes OzEmail in Australia.
Then it is time to sit back and wait ... and wait ... and wait some more.
After 17,132k, my connection to OzEmail stalled for 10 minutes.
The clever Microsoft download program decided to try different sites to resume the process without cutting off the download.
It got me one in Portugal which laboured through the remaining 1800k.
After all that, MSIE 5.0 was finally there in all its glory.
Well not quite. I chose the Standard version. The Deluxe weighs in at 45Mb.
Next time I will wait for the CD-ROM version.
