No acquittals at the television tribunal 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 26-05-1998

 BRISBANE coach John Northey took full responsibility for his side's lamentable loss to Adelaide at home on Saturday night. He made no excuses.
Could we be as honest as Northey, or are there really excuses for any of the following events in the sporting world over the past week?
?Channel 9 for not showing the first state of origin rugby league battle until 12.30am on Saturday; an hour when lovers of the game were probably too tired or emotional, or both, to follow the action.
The game, won 24-23 by Queensland in the last 40 seconds, was rated by commentators as one of the best for years.
?Fremantle supporters for reaching for sedatives whenever Scott Chisholm took the ball in the last quarter of the exciting win over St Kilda at the WACA Ground on Friday night.
Chisholm shone, despite the near heart attacks and his sure work on the goal line almost certainly held off a Saints resurgence.
?Channel 7 for replaying at least five times - in slow motion from every possible angle - Stewart Loewe's freak accident in the same game.
?Tim Watson for identifying Dockers match-winner Matthew Burton as ?Spider Everitt?.
?Ian Robertson for mangling the language and saying ?the bounce is not advantaging? either Fremantle or St Kilda.
?Fremantle's Clive Waterhouse for a mongrel punt from a set shot that missed everything and offended even mongrels.
?Dennis Cometti for saying late in the first quarter of the windy Geelong-West Coast match on Saturday: ?The Eagles by a point (3-2) which is a handy lead in the context of this game.?
?Geelong fans for failing to recognise John Worsfold's 200th game. The crowd, starved of action other than rugby-style rolling mauls, opted to get on the ground for a kick rather than allow Worsfold to be chaired off in style after the gritty 21-point Eagles win.
Of course they may have just wanted to escape the gale-battered stands for fear of flying signs.
?Jason Dunstall, James Hird, Doug Hawkins and Paul Couch for being so obviously nervous on the first night of the disappointing Live and Kicking.
?Kylie Minogue for missing all the celebrity heads when she kicked - surprisingly well - in high heels and a dress so skimpy it appeared to put Hawkins in a dreadful sweat on the same show.
?Viewers who admitted they turned over to Ten's The Panel (where guests actually watched Live and Kicking while waiting to go on).
?Those who say give the show a chance, Couch might say something, Hird may stop interrupting, Hawkins might halve the giggling and Dunstall might stop talking about hairdos (funny subject for a bunch of footy blokes to discuss).
?Sam Newman of Nine's Footy Show for calling Seven's effort Bruised and Bleeding.
?The Footy Show for turning in one of its best efforts this year - despite a noticeable increase in smut, particularly in Street Talk - and being a lot more faithful to football than the wannabee singers and dancers at Seven.
