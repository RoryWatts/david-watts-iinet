Medical care begins at home 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 27-02-1996

 DONKEYS kick, globes spin and an unseen hand writes a letter and puts it in an envelope on the home page of one of the most impressive sites on the World Wide Web.
And it is a wholly West Australian site, run from home by Perth couple Linelle and Bob Griffiths.
The Griffiths are heavily interested in medical service and care - Linelle is a critical care nurse and Bob was with the St John Ambulance service.
Their site reflects these interests with many links to the medical world and a particularly impressive one to the Ying and Yang anti-smoking site.
Mr Griffiths has included a link to the West Australian Ambulance Service and the couple feature on their main link a page about their Balingup farm, Shambalah (a Zulu word meaning safe and peaceful watering hole for all creatures).
This graphic-rich link features more animated kicking donkeys and big graphics of a dog and a donkey ... mainstays of the farm.
Not content with simply providing links to search engines such as Lycos and Yahoo, the Griffiths have built in a multiple search engine into their home page.

ALL THAT'S missing are the Jaffas and chocolate bombs at Lumiere Cinemas' new Web site.
A graphic home page is artwork of the Wellington Street cinemas' foyer with neat signposts to the candy bar, information pillar, box office and the two cinemas.
Clicking on any of these will take the user to different information sites where they are told session times, coming attractions and what is on now.
The site also gives a history of film pioneers the Lumiere brothers, and there is an archive of some of their work.

BUNBURY'S Dolphin Discovery Centre is on the Web with a good package of information about the mammals who are fond of interacting with humans at Koombana Bay.
The site also has links to dolphin information pages around the world.
