PM's page a Net loss 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 01-09-1998

 SORRY, but what election?
To the casual observer on the Internet yesterday, it looked like Prime Minister John Howard had not caught up with word of the October 3 poll.
Mr Howard's official Web site has a page called the PM's Desktop/What's New at which speeches, press releases and transcripts of interviews can be viewed online.
But not a word about the election. The curious omission, however, may be explained by the fact that Mr Howard's site is produced by the Federal Government, not the Liberal Party.
As soon as the poll was announced, the Government started operating in a caretaker role. Convention dictates it has no role in electioneering, therefore, no news of the poll.
The coalition's messages will be sold on the Net through the sites of the Liberal and National parties.
The Liberal Party - whose official site had barely changed since the 1996 victory - added an Election '98 section yesterday.
Federal Labor - whose site has been more active since 1996 and has adopted technology that allows scrolling headlines - announced: ?Australia Deserves Better: 33 Days to a Better Australia?.
Both parties offer Net users the opportunity to read major policies online.
