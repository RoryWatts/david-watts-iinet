Why blokes are such jokes . . . 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 13-02-1998

 LAST year I wrote an awful lot about the difference between the sexes, basically the things that set blokes apart from sheilas. I vowed to myself over the New Year that I would look at a wider range of topics in 1998.
But I lied. So this week you are going to get a list of the things that make being a bloke worthwhile. So there.
Two things inspired me to break the vow. One happened during a recent holiday on Rottnest.
A mate had taken his boys there a couple of days before their GYM (Gorgeous Young Mum . . . see they're back too) could get away from Perth. The bloke and his boys joined up in a cottage with another bloke and his boys, the perfect scenario for a bit of male bonding.
What they achieved, apart from the ability to dodge all the discarded items of clothing and food on the floor, came to light just after the GYM arrived.
She was dozing, half awake, when one of her boys started shouting to some friends at a nearby cottage. To her horror, this is what she heard her son say:
?Hey mate, guess what we did last night? We had a farting competition and I came third.?
Voice of mate: ?Who won??
Boy: ?My dad.?
The second inspiration was the first episode of the alleged new blockbuster sitcom on Channel 9 on Monday, Veronica's Closet. This stars the entirely gorgeous and cuddly Kirstie Alley as the head of a fashion empire. Unfortunately she is about twice the girl she was when she was a glamorous model, and has a cheating, no-good bloke of a husband, who, after committing adultery, tries to lure her back by singing some awful love ballad.
Anyway, Veronica's answer to these mini-tragedies is to gorge on junk food, then to diet, then to binge, then to diet etc etc . . . the never-ending cycle of despair.
The show, like so much junk entertainment from the US and that includes the film Titanic, centres on stereotyping. If a woman is not a thin, blonde bimbo in a tight dress, she has to stuff her face instead of having sex.
For example, not one of the heartless but stunning bitches in Melrose Place ever resorts to a doughnut or milkshake. They are too busy scheming and plotting their way into the wrong man's bed.
But the Veronicas of this world, disguised in flowing kaftans that hide their ample curves, are forced into a fixation about weight. Thin equals sex; fat equals junk food . . . for women.
WHICH leads us to this little, edited (because there are some rude words around) list of the best things about being a bloke. This list originated on the Internet, like a growing number of modern phenomena like Bill Clinton's sex life (why do we need to know so much?). Here is the list:
1. Phone conversations are over in 30 seconds flat.
2. Movie nudity is virtually always female.
3. You know stuff about tanks.
4. A five-day holiday requires only one suitcase.
5. Queues to public toilets at major events are 80 per cent shorter.
6. Old friends don't give a fig whether you've lost or gained weight.
7. Your bottom is never a factor in job interviews.
8. You don't have to lug a bag of useless stuff around everywhere you go.
9. You can go to the toilet without a support group.
10. You can kill your own food.
11. You can be showered and ready to go in 10 minutes.
12. If someone forgets to invite you to something, he or she can still be your friend.
13. You can buy three pieces of underwear for $10.
14. If you're 34 and single, nobody notices.
15. Three pairs of shoes are more than enough.
FHOW about a little joke? This was told to me by my mate Bill from Daglish, who honestly is old enough to know better.
Q: What do you call a bear without ears?
A: A B.
