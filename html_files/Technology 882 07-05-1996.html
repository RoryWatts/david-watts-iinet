Internet stirred up by gun-control arguments 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 07-05-1996

 THE senseless slaughter of 35 people with high-powered rifles at once serene Port Arthur is dominating debate on the Internet.
Gun control is the hot topic in the aus.politics and aus.general newsgroups, which had slumped into torpor after the Federal election.
Arguments about whether guns should be banned totally, Australian laws made uniform and even the necessity of control are appearing hourly under a variety of headings.
Debate started within hours of the first news of the tragedy on April 29.
A correspondent wrote to aus.politics saying that if people had been allowed to carry concealed hand-guns, the killings would never have reached such dreadful proportions because one of the eventual victims could have shot and killed or wounded the gunman.
He backed the statement with literature from the US saying gun control ?enslaved the population?.
People who replied to the posting were adamant that gun control was necessary, citing figures that showed the US - with weapons freely available in most States - had a murder rate per 100,000 young males aged 15-24 of 37.2, compared with 0.09 in neighbouring Canada, which has strict gun laws.
There appears to be no uniform conclusion but some postings are becoming vitriolic with correspondents resorting to name-calling and insults.
This a fairly mild example of the vindictiveness of the debate.
Pro-gun correspondent: ?This is a discussion? Go to bed, Mark.?
Another pro-gunner: ?How old is Mark anyway? I'm beginning to get the impression that he's only 12 or 13. Well, that does seem to be his mental age. Gee I hope he doesn't actually own a gun - now that is a scary thought.?
A wide variety of sites on the World Wide Web tackle gun control, although it is hard to find any directly related to Port Arthur.
The Ban Guns site is one that was set up in direct response to the massacre and is an online petition to Prime Minister John Howard calling for a complete gun ban.
The unnamed creator of the site wrote: ?Politicians are continually attempting to change the topic when it comes to gun laws in order to "keep the peace' with members of the gun lobby. I believe it is about time the real voice of opinion on this matter was heard. I support the movement to BAN guns in Australia. Not register, not licence, but BAN to all except civil defence and law enforcement.?
Other Web sites, principally in the US, raise issues for and against, and information from these sites is often quoted in the newsgroup arguments.
