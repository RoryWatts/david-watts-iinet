Fans expect dickens of a fortnight 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 27-07-1999

 THE next two episodes of Great Expectations will make enthralling viewing.
Those who thought the ABC adaptation of Charles Dickens' classic finished on Sunday night are correct.
But the Channel 7 version for supporters of the Eagles and Dockers is still running.
The first episode next weekend will be shown on Saturday night under the title ?Surely the Eagles are not that bad?.
The sub-title is ?They couldn't lose to Collingwood ... could they ... no seriously?.
A less dramatic but equally important episode will screen at noon on Sunday.
It is called ?Dockers say farewell to 15th spot?.
The old script for Great Expectations went like this back in June, just after the Kangaroos had beaten the Eagles by 20 points - win the next five, put down the Brisbane game at the Gabba as a 50-50 bet, thump Collingwood, give Essendon a fright but probably lose, then win the next three to finish first or second with the certain prospect of two home finals.
Richmond did not like that script and Fremantle thought it stank.
Brisbane just laughed and tore it up.
The new script is available strictly on a week-by-week basis.
But if fans of Great Expectations had listened to Mick Malthouse properly all year, instead of being beguiled by the six-nil start, they might have been prepared for some changes in plot.
After West Coast thrashed Essendon by 74 points in round four, he said the club was still on a rebuilding program.
Not too much should be expected of it yet.
Bomber coach Kevin Sheedy retorted: ?I think Mick's a great comic.?
Even after his team defeated Geelong in round 13, and was still on top of the ladder, Malthouse refused to join the ranks of Great Expectations followers.
?Let's worry about the eight before worrying about how many wins it takes to make the top four,? he said.
And now the plot centres on whether the Eagles can beat Collingwood, the last-placed side; Collingwood which has not beaten West Coast in Perth since 1992 (though that was at the WACA ground).
History might help viewers understand the next Eagles episode better.
How does the club react after a thrashing?
After its biggest loss - by 142 points to Essendon in 1989 - it won the next three straight.
After the 130-point loss to Sydney in 1987 it won the next four out of six.
Under Malthouse, in the 1992 premiership year, it scored only 3.11 when losing to St Kilda by 66 points in round 18 but then won the next three in a row, including a 131-point slaughter of Brisbane.
In 1995, its worst defeat of 62 points was inflicted by North in round 18, but it won the next three of four.
Importantly, after succumbing to North, it kicked 26.21 while rampaging over Adelaide by 135 points in the very next game.
If other sub-plots work this weekend - Richmond beats the Kangaroos, and Carlton defeats the Bulldogs - West Coast could even be in second spot ... provided it beats Collingwood. And a Fremantle win might lift it as high as 12th.
How's that for Great Expectations?
