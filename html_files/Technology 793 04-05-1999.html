Joker deals top deck to help Net novices 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 04-05-1999

 THE WEB
Top Sites collection
www.southwest.com.au/~joker/
NEWCOMERS to the Web are often baffled by the staggering amount of information out there in cyberspace.
A 22-year-old student and freelance technology consultant from Mandurah believes he has an answer.
Jason - shy about his full name and better known on the Web as Joker - has created a super site from his bookmarks designed to help ease the pain for newbies navigating the medium.
?I found that a lot of people didn't know what there was on the Internet, and I didn't want to carry around my bookmark file, so I created a site containing only the best of my bookmarks,? Jason said.
The result is called Top Sites and it assembles the best that Jason has come across.
At the moment about 300 sites are filed in easily-followed categories - business, computers, multimedia etc - but Jason has set a target of 2500 places to surf and is encouraging visitors to recommend inclusions.
People can even download a copy of the Top Sites links to their hard drives for faster retrieval by following simple instructions.
Jason's belief is not only do newbies find it baffling to search the Web, but they have particular difficulty in finding good Australian sites.
Early last week he had compiled more than 90 home-grown sites in a special category to help ease this pain.
FOOD
ChowNow - Order Asian food online
www.chownow.com.au
THE idea of ordering pizza online hit Perth earlier this year. Last Friday night it became possible to order Asian takeaway as well. ChowNow is a creation of PizzaNow's inventors, AusWeb, and another brainchild of Web developer Brian Gow.
It allows surfers to register for free and find their nearest Asian takeaway. Within a few clicks a hungry surfer can order from a menu and send an e-mail to the takeaway. The order and the satisfaction come the usual way - by delivery boy to the door.
SPORT
Netball Australia
www.netball.asn.au
NETBALL is a sport Australian women lead the world in. The development of this site suggests our netballing nuts are also well up when it comes to Web technology.
Netball Australia is powered by Flash technology which delivers a vibrant and colourful welcome to the site with moving images of a court and the sounds of whistles and cheering.
The site provides links to news, questionnaires, merchandise information, a calendar of major fixtures, State-based resources and a history of the game.
