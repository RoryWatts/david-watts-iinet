Internet a sad lonely votes club 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 01-03-1996

 NO ONE loves me. Paul Keating answered my mail but that was the end of the relationship. Johhny Howard did not bother to write.
What have I done? Am I really so on the nose as to be an untouchable.
After all, it was their fault.
They - or people in the Labor and Liberal parties unknown - said I could take part in democracy of a new-fangled electronic kind on the Internet.
Tempting me was the chance to have the great debaters answer questions on-line about Australia's future in cyberspace.
At least Mr Keating - or perhaps an electronically literate henchman - had the good grace to talk to me.
Within 24 hours, there was the reply: ?Dear David, thank you for your e-mail. It might be quicker to send your questions via editor@alp.org.au.?
I rushed the questions through ... four days later still no response.
Mr Howard was a different matter.
The Liberals had been crowing about how their World Wide Web site had an electronic contact for every candidate.
There was space to write to the candidate of your choice all right. But all mail went to the same address.
I nonetheless posted Mr Howard my request to ask questions.
That was Monday.
The lackey who took my e-mail is perhaps still trying to catch Mr Howard.
