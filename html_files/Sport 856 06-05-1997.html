Get in some Net practice for watching Ashes tour 

Author: DAVID WATTS

Date: 06-05-1997

 NO ONE seriously believes the England cricket team has a hope of regaining the Ashes from Australia.
Mark Taylor's super-confident team begins its campaign next week on the playing fields of England. But different cricketing campaigns are already being waged on the World Wide Web.
Possibly the most delirious is an English site called How England will regain the Ashes.
The recipe is very tongue-in-cheek and includes putting former England captain and known trencherman Mike Gatting in charge of catering.
The site is rumour-ridden - did you know Shane Warne is returning to Australia after the first three Tests are safely sewn up? - but lots of fun and even has links to a few serious sporting pages.
On the serious side of a serious game is the worthy Baggy Green site's Australia v England pages.
This vast database has the result of every Test between the two countries since 1877 as well as the statistical delights of highest wicket takers and greatest run makers.
Sir Len Hutton's 364 remains the highest Ashes score, but a man named Bradman is responsible for nearly half the next 14 highest scores.
Sir Don is a prolific figure on the Web. There is even a homage site in Canada. But the highest praise can be found at Australian sites, the CricInfo mirror and the State Library of South Australia's Don Bradman collection.
More modern cricket heroes can be found easily. The Steve Waugh is God page pays tribute to our number one batsman.
