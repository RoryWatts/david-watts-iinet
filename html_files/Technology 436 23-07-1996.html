It's been a hard day's search ... 

Author: DAVID WATTS; NATHAN COCHRANE

Date: 23-07-1996

 SURFING the Net is fun if you know what you are looking for. But that is not always the case.
There seem to be as many search engines as topics and if a Web surfer is not in the right mood, then they can easily revert to the real world - turn off the computer and go outside.
The US Internet giant c:net has taken a poke at the frustrating side of searching in its latest newsletter to subscribers by listing a mostly mythical top-10 songs for Net users.
Here they are in reverse order:
10. I Got Yahoo, Babe (Sonny and Cher)
9. I'm So Excite, Inc. (The Pointer Sisters)
8. Spies Lycos (Paul McCartney)
7. I Can Seek Queries Now (Johnny Nash)
6. Boolean Rhapsody (Queen)
5. Go (Perl Jam)
4. I Can CGI for Miles and Miles (The Who)
3. Do Inktomi One More Time (The Captain and Tenille)
2. Inagaddaltavista (Iron Butterfly)
1. I Still Haven't Found What I'm Looking For (U2)

POP the corks and down the champers, Australian Web software company, Sausage Software, turns one this Sunday and the party is on them.
More than 60 cybercafes across the world, including two in Fremantle, will play host to Australia's biggest cyber-rave.
The online party will be held on both the World Wide Web and Internet Relay Chat.
Surfers will be able to chat directly to each other on a wide variety of topics including Java, Active X, making money on the Net, and new products.
Surfers will also be able to win prizes.
Sausage Software has grown from a one-man operation last year, to a company employing more than 50 full-time staff.
Hot Dog is Australia's leading supplier of Web-related programs, including the company's flagship product, Hot Dog.
Earlier this month, company founder Steve Outtrim announced plans to issue shares in the fast growing company.
Projected revenues for the financial year just ended are $3 million.
The cyber-rave kicks off this Sunday from 10am at Net Trek and Cafe Aria.
- Nathan Cochrane
